## Lease Agreement Basics
### Key Terms to Review
- **Rent amount and due date**
- **Lease term**: Month-to-month or fixed term
- **Security deposit**: Amount and terms
- **Utilities**: What's included
- **Pets**: Allowed? Deposits? Fees?
- **Maintenance responsibilities**
- **Guest policies**
- **Subletting rules**
- **Notice requirements**

### Red Flags
- Automatic renewal without notice
- Excessive late fees
- Landlord can enter anytime
- Waiver of legal rights
- Unreasonable rules
- Rent increases mid-lease
- Non-refundable deposits (many states prohibit)

## Security Deposits
### General Rules (Vary by State)
- Typically 1-2 months' rent max
- Must be returned within 14-60 days (state-specific)
- Itemized deduction list required
- Interest payment (some states)
- Separate account requirement (some states)

### Deductions Allowed
- Unpaid rent
- Damage beyond normal wear and tear
- Cleaning (if left excessively dirty)
- Unpaid utilities (if responsible)

### Normal Wear and Tear (Not Deductible)
- Faded paint
- Worn carpet (expected lifespan)
- Small nail holes
- Minor scuffs on walls
- Worn fixtures from normal use

### Getting Deposit Back
- Document condition at move-in (photos/video)
- Clean thoroughly before move-out
- Repair any damage you caused
- Provide forwarding address
- Request walk-through
- Send demand letter if wrongfully withheld

## Repairs and Habitability
### Landlord's Responsibilities (Warranty of Habitability)
- Safe structure (roof, walls, stairs)
- Working heat and hot water
- Functional plumbing and electricity
- No pest infestations
- Working locks and security
- Smoke/carbon monoxide detectors
- No mold or health hazards
- Trash removal (in multi-unit buildings)

### How to Request Repairs
1. **Written notice**: Email or letter (keep copy)
2. **Reasonable time**: Give landlord time to fix (7-30 days depending on severity)
3. **Follow up**: If not fixed, send second notice
4. **Document**: Photos, communications
5. **Next steps**: Withhold rent, repair and deduct, or call inspectors

### Rent Withholding (State-Specific!)
- Serious habitability issues only
- Written notice to landlord first
- Escrow rent or keep separately
- Follow specific state procedures
- Risk of eviction if done wrong

### Repair and Deduct
- Fix issue yourself
- Deduct cost from rent
- Usually limited (1-2 months' rent or less)
- Proper notice required
- Keep receipts

## Eviction Process
### Legal Reasons for Eviction
- Non-payment of rent
- Lease violation
- End of lease term (no-fault)
- Owner move-in (some jurisdictions)
- Illegal activity

### Eviction Process
1. **Notice**: 3-day (pay or quit), 30/60-day (no cause)
2. **Court filing**: If tenant doesn't comply
3. **Court hearing**: Tenant can defend
4. **Judgment**: Court decides
5. **Sheriff eviction**: If landlord wins
6. **Cannot self-evict**: Changing locks, shutting utilities illegal

### Defenses to Eviction
- Landlord failed to maintain property
- Eviction is retaliatory (for complaint)
- Eviction is discriminatory
- Improper notice
- Rent was paid on time
- Lease violation dispute

### Never Do This
- Stop paying rent without legal cause
- Abandon property
- Ignore court summons
- Let landlord evict without fighting (if unjust)

## Rent Control & Stabilization
### Rent Control
- Limits on rent increases
- Specific cities/jurisdictions
- Often exempts new construction
- Just cause eviction protection
- Check local laws

### Rent Stabilization
- Less restrictive than control
- Some increase limits
- More common than control

### Knowing Your Rights
- Research local ordinances
- Tenant unions and advocacy groups
- Legal aid organizations
- City housing departments

## Privacy and Entry Rights
### Landlord Entry Rules
- **Notice required**: Typically 24-48 hours
- **Reasonable hours**: Usually 8 AM - 6 PM
- **Valid reasons**: Repairs, inspections, showings
- **Emergency**: Can enter without notice

### Tenant Rights
- Can refuse entry without proper notice (except emergency)
- Can be present during entry
- Can document landlord harassment
- Can change locks (with landlord permission/providing key)

## Discrimination and Fair Housing
### Protected Classes (Federal)
- Race, color, national origin
- Religion
- Sex (including sexual harassment)
- Familial status (children)
- Disability

### State/Local Additions
- Sexual orientation
- Gender identity
- Source of income (Section 8)
- Age (some states)
- Others (varies)

### Illegal Actions
- Refusing to rent based on protected class
- Different terms or conditions
- Harassment
- Steering to certain units
- Refusing reasonable accommodations (disability)

## Breaking a Lease
### Legal Reasons (Usually Allowed)
- Uninhabitable conditions
- Landlord harassment
- Domestic violence victim (many states)
- Military deployment (SCRA)
- Landlord breach of lease

### Without Legal Cause
- May owe remaining rent
- Early termination fees (if in lease)
- Landlord duty to mitigate (must try to re-rent)
- Can negotiate with landlord
- Subletting (if allowed)

### How to Do It
- Review lease for early termination clause
- Written notice to landlord
- Offer to help find new tenant
- Document your reasons
- Pay what you legally owe
- Get everything in writing
