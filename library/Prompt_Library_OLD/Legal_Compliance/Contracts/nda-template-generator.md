# NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) Template Generator

Generate customized non-disclosure agreements for various business scenarios.

## Prompt

```
Create a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) for:

**Type:** [Mutual/Unilateral]
**Parties:**
- Disclosing Party: [Company/Individual Name]
- Receiving Party: [Company/Individual Name]

**Purpose:** [Why confidential information is being shared]
**Duration:** [Time period of confidentiality obligation]
**Jurisdiction:** [State/Country]

**Include these sections:**

1. **Definition of Confidential Information**
   - What is considered confidential
   - Exceptions (publicly known, independently developed, etc.)

2. **Obligations of Receiving Party**
   - Use only for specified purpose
   - Maintain confidentiality
   - Limit access to need-to-know basis
   - Return or destroy information upon request

3. **Exclusions from Confidential Information**
   - Information already public
   - Information independently developed
   - Information rightfully obtained from third party
   - Information disclosed with written consent

4. **Term and Termination**
   - Effective date
   - Duration of agreement
   - Survival of obligations after termination

5. **Remedies**
   - Injunctive relief provision
   - Damages
   - Attorney's fees

6. **Miscellaneous**
   - Governing law
   - Jurisdiction
   - Severability
   - Entire agreement
   - Amendment process
   - Signature blocks

**⚠️ Legal Disclaimer:**
This is a template for informational purposes. Consult with a licensed attorney in your jurisdiction before using. Laws vary by state/country.
```

## Common Use Cases

**Mutual NDA:** Both parties share confidential information
- Partnership discussions
- Joint ventures
- Collaborative projects

**Unilateral NDA:** One party shares, other receives
- Hiring contractors/employees
- Investor pitches
- Vendor/supplier relationships

## Key Considerations

**Duration:** Typical is 2-5 years
**Scope:** Be specific about what's confidential
**Carve-outs:** Allow for legally required disclosures
**Return/Destruction:** Specify what happens to materials after termination
