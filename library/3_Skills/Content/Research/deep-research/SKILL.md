# Deep Research

## Overview
Produce cited research reports from multiple sources. Use this skill when the user needs investigation, competitive analysis, market research, technology evaluation, or current-state synthesis with clear source attribution.

## When to Use
- The user asks to research, investigate, compare, or deep dive into a topic.
- The answer requires current information or multiple independent sources.
- A decision depends on evidence rather than general background knowledge.
- The output should include citations, methodology, confidence, and gaps.

## Research Workflow
1. Clarify the goal, audience, decision, timeframe, and required depth when those details are missing.
2. Break the topic into 3-5 sub-questions.
3. Search multiple query variants for each sub-question.
4. Prioritize primary sources, official documentation, academic papers, reputable news, and credible industry analysis.
5. Read the strongest sources in depth rather than relying only on snippets.
6. Synthesize findings into themes, separating facts from estimates and inference.
7. Include sources, methodology, and unresolved gaps.

## Report Structure
```markdown
# [Topic]: Research Report

Generated: [date]
Sources reviewed: [N]
Confidence: High | Medium | Low

## Executive Summary
[3-5 sentences with the strongest findings.]

## Key Findings
1. [Theme with citation]
2. [Theme with citation]
3. [Theme with citation]

## Analysis
### [Theme 1]
[Evidence, nuance, and implications.]

### [Theme 2]
[Evidence, nuance, and implications.]

## Gaps and Uncertainties
- [What could not be verified.]

## Sources
1. [Title](url) - one-line relevance note

## Methodology
Searched [queries] across [source types]. Read [N] sources in depth.
```

## Quality Rules
- Cite every factual claim that is not common knowledge.
- Cross-check important claims against more than one source.
- Prefer recent sources when recency changes the answer.
- Flag single-source claims as unverified.
- State when there is insufficient data.
- Do not fabricate URLs, quotes, numbers, or source names.
