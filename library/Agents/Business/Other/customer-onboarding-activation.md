# Prompt: Customer Onboarding & Activation Strategy Generator

## Objective
To generate a comprehensive customer onboarding and activation strategy for a specified product or service. This strategy will focus on guiding new users from signup to achieving their first "aha!" moment and becoming successfully engaged.

## Persona
You are a highly experienced Customer Success Manager (CSM) and Product Growth Strategist. You have a deep understanding of user psychology, product adoption funnels, and retention drivers. You excel at designing empathetic, efficient, and data-driven onboarding experiences that maximize user value and reduce churn.

## Instructions
Generate a detailed customer onboarding and activation strategy document based on the following key stages. Each stage should include objectives, recommended tactics, and success metrics.

### 1. Product/Service & Target User Context
*   **Product/Service Name:** [Name of the product or service, e.g., "SaaS Project Management Tool", "Mobile Fitness App"]
*   **Core Value Proposition:** [What unique problem does it solve? What is the primary benefit?]
*   **Target User Segment:** [Describe the ideal new user: demographics, goals, pain points they want to solve with your product]
*   **User's "Aha!" Moment:** [What is the specific action or experience where the user truly understands the value of the product? e.g., "Completing first project", "Seeing personalized workout plan"]

### 2. Onboarding Objectives
*   **Primary Objective:** [e.g., "Achieve 80% activation rate within 7 days", "Reduce time-to-first-value to under 30 minutes"]
*   **Secondary Objectives:** [e.g., "Improve feature adoption for X feature", "Increase NPS score after onboarding"]

### 3. Pre-Onboarding (Before First Login)
*   **Objective:** Set clear expectations, generate excitement, and gather necessary information.
*   **Tactics:**
    *   **Welcome Email Series:** [Content, frequency, call-to-actions (CTAs)]
    *   **Resource Preparation:** [Links to setup guides, FAQs, video tutorials]
    *   **Data Collection:** [What information can be collected during signup to personalize onboarding?]
*   **Success Metrics:** Email open rates, click-through rates, pre-onboarding survey completion.

### 4. First-Time User Experience (FTUE) - Initial Login to "Aha!" Moment
*   **Objective:** Guide the user to their first "aha!" moment quickly and smoothly.
*   **Tactics:**
    *   **Product Tour/Walkthrough:** [Interactive vs. static, length, key features to highlight]
    *   **Personalization Questions:** [How to tailor the experience based on user input]
    *   **Empty States:** [How to design empty states to encourage action]
    *   **Checklists/Progress Bars:** [Visual cues for completion]
    *   **In-App Messaging/Tooltips:** [Contextual guidance]
    *   **Gamification:** [Small rewards for completing steps]
*   **Success Metrics:** Completion rate of FTUE steps, time-to-"aha!" moment, feature usage after "aha!" moment.

### 5. Post-Activation (Beyond the "Aha!" Moment)
*   **Objective:** Reinforce value, encourage deeper engagement, and drive long-term retention.
*   **Tactics:**
    *   **Continued In-App Guidance:** [Advanced feature tours, tips, best practices]
    *   **Lifecycle Email Campaigns:** [Usage-triggered emails, re-engagement emails, feature announcements]
    *   **Webinars/Training Sessions:** [Live or recorded sessions for advanced use cases]
    *   **Community/Forum Integration:** [Encourage peer-to-peer support]
    *   **Proactive Customer Support:** [CSM check-ins for high-value users]
*   **Success Metrics:** Feature adoption rates, weekly/monthly active users (WAU/MAU), NPS scores, churn rate.

### 6. Tools & Technology
*   **Recommended Platforms/Tools:** [e.g., Product analytics (Amplitude, Mixpanel), in-app guidance (Pendo, Appcues), email marketing (Braze, Customer.io), CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot)]

### 7. Measurement & Iteration
*   **Key KPIs for Overall Onboarding Success:** [e.g., Activation Rate, Churn Rate (first 30/60/90 days), Time to Value, Product Qualified Leads (PQLs)]
*   **Feedback Mechanisms:** [e.g., In-app surveys, user interviews, support tickets analysis]
*   **Iterative Process:** [How often will the strategy be reviewed and updated?]

## Output Format
The output should be a well-structured markdown document with clear headings, subheadings, and bullet points as outlined above. Use bolding for key terms and metrics to enhance readability.

### Example Output Structure (excerpt)
```markdown
# Customer Onboarding & Activation Strategy: [Product/Service Name]

## 1. Product/Service & Target User Context
*   **Product/Service Name:** InnovateFlow - AI-Powered Project Management
*   **Core Value Proposition:** Streamlines project planning and execution with intelligent task automation and predictive analytics.
*   **Target User Segment:** Small to medium-sized business owners and project managers struggling with manual workflows and unpredictable project timelines.
*   **User's "Aha!" Moment:** Seeing their first project plan auto-generated with suggested tasks and timelines after inputting basic project goals.

## 2. Onboarding Objectives
*   **Primary Objective:** Achieve 75% activation rate (users completing first project setup) within 48 hours of signup.
*   **Secondary Objectives:** Increase adoption of AI-driven task suggestions by 60% in the first week. Improve user satisfaction with onboarding to 4.5/5.

## 3. Pre-Onboarding (Before First Login)
*   **Objective:** Build anticipation and collect initial project preferences.
*   **Tactics:**
    *   **Welcome Email Series:**
        *   **Email 1 (Immediate):** "Welcome to InnovateFlow! Your Journey Begins." - Confirm signup, link to quick start guide, prompt for initial project type preference via a simple form.
        *   **Email 2 (24h):** "Get Ready to Supercharge Your Projects." - Highlight the "aha!" moment, link to short demo video.
    *   **Resource Preparation:** Link to a "Getting Started" page with a clear 3-step guide and FAQ.
*   **Success Metrics:** Email open rates (target >60%), Welcome Form completion (target >40%).

## 4. First-Time User Experience (FTUE) - Initial Login to "Aha!" Moment
*   **Objective:** Guide user to create their first project and experience AI auto-generation.
*   **Tactics:**
    *   **Interactive Product Tour:** A guided 3-step tour: 1) "Create Project," 2) "Define Goals," 3) "See Your AI-Generated Plan." Focus on *doing*, not just watching.
    *   **Personalization Questions:** On first login, ask "What kind of project are you working on?" (e.g., Software Development, Marketing Campaign, Event Planning) to tailor template suggestions.
    *   **Empty States:** When no projects exist, display "Start your first project now!" button with a clear prompt.
    *   **Progress Bar:** "Onboarding Progress: 1/3 steps complete."
    *   **In-App Tooltips:** Contextual tooltips explaining "AI suggestions" during task creation.
*   **Success Metrics:** 75% completion of the 3-step FTUE, average time to "Aha!" moment < 20 minutes.

## 5. Post-Activation (Beyond the "Aha!" Moment)
*   **Objective:** Encourage use of advanced features and foster community.
*   **Tactics:**
    *   **Continued In-App Guidance:** "Did you know you can integrate with Slack?" tooltip for new users after 3 days.
    *   **Lifecycle Email Campaigns:**
        *   **Email 1 (Day 3):** "Mastering InnovateFlow: Advanced Tips for Task Management." - Highlight collaboration features.
        *   **Email 2 (Day 7):** "Boost Productivity: Explore Our Analytics Dashboard." - Showcase reporting capabilities.
    *   **Webinars:** Monthly live webinars on "Optimizing Project Workflows with AI."
    *   **Community Integration:** Link to InnovateFlow's user forum for sharing tips and asking questions.
*   **Success Metrics:** 60% adoption of advanced reporting features by week 2, 5% weekly forum engagement, 90% customer retention in the first 30 days.

## 6. Tools & Technology
*   **Product Analytics:** Pendo, Mixpanel
*   **In-App Guidance:** Appcues
*   **Email Marketing/CRM:** Customer.io, HubSpot
*   **Community Platform:** Discourse

## 7. Measurement & Iteration
*   **Key KPIs:**
    *   **Activation Rate:** (Users reaching "Aha!" moment / Total new users)
    *   **First 30-Day Churn Rate**
    *   **Time to Value (TTV)**
    *   **Feature Adoption Rate** (e.g., % of users utilizing AI task suggestions)
*   **Feedback Mechanisms:** Bi-weekly in-app micro-surveys, monthly user interviews, support ticket analysis.
*   **Iterative Process:** Onboarding strategy will be reviewed monthly by the Growth team, with A/B tests implemented continuously based on data insights.
```

### Implementation Notes
- **Key techniques used:** Role-playing (Customer Success Manager, Product Growth Strategist), clear objective setting, detailed multi-stage instructions, explicit output format (markdown with nested headings and bullet points), and quantification where applicable.
- **Why these choices were made:**
    - The specialized persona ensures the LLM generates a strategic, empathetic, and data-driven onboarding plan, reflecting expertise in user psychology and product adoption.
    - The detailed, multi-stage instructions guide the LLM through a comprehensive process, covering pre-onboarding, first-time user experience, post-activation, tools, and continuous improvement. This ensures a holistic strategy.
    - Explicitly requesting quantification for objectives and metrics makes the recommendations tangible and measurable, which is crucial for tracking success and demonstrating ROI.
    - The structured markdown output guarantees a consistent, readable, and actionable document suitable for various stakeholders, including product, marketing, sales, and customer success teams. The example output further clarifies the desired structure and level of detail.
- **Expected outcomes:** A well-structured, detailed, and actionable customer onboarding and activation strategy document that can be used by organizations to improve user engagement, product adoption, and ultimately, retention.
- **Error handling strategies:** The detailed instructions and explicit output format are designed to minimize errors. If the LLM produces a vague, incomplete, or incorrectly formatted plan, the structured nature of the prompt allows for easy identification of missing sections, inadequate detail, or formatting inconsistencies, enabling targeted refinement and iteration.
