# Cybersecurity Threat Advisory Email

## Purpose
Draft an urgent, authoritative, yet reassuring email to clients informing them of a new, widely publicized cybersecurity threat or vulnerability (e.g., a zero-day exploit) and the steps being taken to protect them.

## Instructions
Act as the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) or Lead Security Engineer for a cybersecurity vendor or IT consultancy. Write a threat advisory email to be sent to all active clients. The email must balance urgency with calm assurance. It needs to clearly explain the threat in plain English, outline exactly what your company is doing to mitigate it, and state whether the client needs to take any immediate action.

Specify the name/type of the vulnerability (e.g., Log4j, a major data breach at a common third-party vendor), the potential impact, and the required client action (if any).

## Output Format
Structure the threat advisory as follows:
1. **Subject Line**: Needs to be clear, urgent, and easily identifiable (e.g., "URGENT SECURITY ADVISORY:").
2. **The Situation (What happened?)**: A brief, non-alarmist summary of the vulnerability or threat.
3. **The Impact (Does this affect us?)**: Clearly stating whether the client's systems are currently known to be vulnerable.
4. **Our Action Plan (What are we doing?)**: Bullet points detailing the steps your team has already taken (e.g., patching, scanning, monitoring).
5. **Client Action Required (What do you need to do?)**: Clear instructions for the client (e.g., "No action required," "Please update X software," "Reset passwords").
6. **Support Contact Info**: Dedicated channels for emergency questions.

## Example
**Input:**
Vulnerability: A critical zero-day vulnerability in a widely used VPN client (e.g., "VPN-X").
Potential Impact: Unauthorized remote access to corporate networks.
Client Action: Require users to restart their VPN clients to apply the forced patch.

**Output:**
### 1. Subject Line
URGENT SECURITY ADVISORY: Action Required Regarding VPN-X Vulnerability

### 2. The Situation
Dear [Name],
Over the past 24 hours, a critical vulnerability was disclosed affecting the [VPN-X] client, which is used by many organizations to facilitate secure remote work. If exploited, this vulnerability could allow unauthorized access to internal networks.

### 3. The Impact
Our initial scans indicate that your organization utilizes this software. However, we currently have no evidence of any active exploitation within your environment.

*(Continues with Our Action Plan, Client Action Required, and Support Contact Info)*
