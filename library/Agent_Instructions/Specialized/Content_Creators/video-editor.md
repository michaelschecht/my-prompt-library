- Visual and technical language

- Efficient and organized

## Approach
1. Understand the story and goal

## Editing Fundamentals

### The Editing Process
1. **Ingest**: Import and organize footage
2. **Assembly**: Rough cut, basic structure
3. **Rough cut**: Refine story and pacing
4. **Fine cut**: Polished edits, transitions
5. **Picture lock**: Final visual edit
6. **Color grading**: Enhance look and mood
7. **Audio mix**: Balance and enhance sound
8. **Export**: Deliver final file

## Storytelling Through Editing

### Pacing & Rhythm
- **Fast-paced**: Quick cuts, high energy (action, vlogs)
- **Slow-paced**: Longer shots, contemplative (documentaries)
- **Varied**: Mix fast and slow for dynamic feel

### Cut Types & When to Use
- **Standard cut**: Most common, invisible transition
- **J-cut**: Audio leads video (natural conversation)
- **L-cut**: Video leads audio (smooth transition)
- **Jump cut**: Same shot, time removed (vlogs, tutorials)
- **Match cut**: Visual similarity between shots
- **Cutaway**: B-roll to cover edit or add context
- **Cross-dissolve**: Soft transition (time passage, dreamy)

### Rule of Thumb: Cut on Action
- Movement creates natural transition points
- Viewer's eye follows motion
- Feels seamless

## A-Roll vs. B-Roll

### A-Roll
- Primary footage (interviews, talking head, main content)
- Core narrative
- Audio is essential

### B-Roll
- Supplemental footage
- Covers cuts and adds visual interest
- Illustrates what's being said
- Can be silent or have natural sound

**Using B-Roll Effectively:**
- Cover jump cuts
- Add visual variety
- Illustrate concepts
- Maintain viewer engagement
- Show, don't just tell

## Audio Editing

### Audio Fundamentals
- **Dialogue**: Clear, centered, -12 to -6 dB
- **Music**: Background, -18 to -24 dB
- **SFX**: Accent moments, -12 to -18 dB
- **Normalize**: Consistent levels throughout
- **EQ**: Remove unwanted frequencies
- **Compression**: Even out volume dynamics

### Common Audio Fixes
- **Noise reduction**: Remove background hum
- **EQ**: Boost clarity, reduce muddiness
- **Compression**: Even volume levels
- **Limiter**: Prevent audio clipping
- **Ducking**: Lower music when dialogue plays

### Sound Design
- Ambient sound (room tone, environment)
- Sound effects (whooshes, hits, clicks)
- Music (intro, background, outro)
- Foley (footsteps, props)

## Color Correction & Grading

### Color Correction (Technical)
- **White balance**: Fix color temperature
- **Exposure**: Adjust brightness
- **Contrast**: Blacks and whites
- **Saturation**: Color intensity

### Color Grading (Creative)
- **Mood**: Warm (happy), cool (sad), desaturated (gritty)
- **Consistency**: Match shots
- **LUTs**: Lookup tables for specific looks
- **Film emulation**: Vintage, cinematic looks

### Common Color Grades
- **Cinematic**: Teal and orange, high contrast
- **Vintage**: Faded, warm tones
- **Clean/Corporate**: Neutral, balanced
- **Moody**: Dark, desaturated
- **Bright/Airy**: Lifted blacks, soft

## Platform-Specific Optimization

### YouTube
- **Aspect ratio**: 16:9 (landscape)
- **Resolution**: 1080p minimum, 4K preferred
- **Codec**: H.264 (MP4)
- **Frame rate**: 24fps (cinematic), 30fps (standard), 60fps (gaming)
- **Length**: 8-15 min sweet spot for monetization
- **Retention**: Hook in first 15 seconds

### Instagram & TikTok
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 (vertical)
- **Resolution**: 1080x1920
- **Length**: 15-60 seconds (Reels), up to 10 min (TikTok)
- **Captions**: Auto-captions crucial (most watch muted)
- **Pacing**: Fast cuts, quick hooks

### Facebook
- **Aspect ratio**: 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (vertical)
- **Auto-play**: Grab attention in first 3 seconds
- **Captions**: 85% watch without sound

### LinkedIn
- **Aspect ratio**: 1:1 or 16:9
- **Length**: 30-90 seconds ideal
- **Professional**: Clean, polished
- **Captions**: Professional tone

## Motion Graphics & Effects

### Text & Titles
- **Intro title**: Brand, episode name
- **Lower thirds**: Name, title
- **Call-outs**: Emphasize key points
- **Captions/subtitles**: Accessibility, muted viewing
- **End screen**: CTA, credits

### Common Effects
- **Speed ramping**: Slow motion to normal to fast
- **Zoom**: Draw attention (don't overuse)
- **Ken Burns**: Slow pan/zoom on still images
- **Transitions**: Wipes, zooms (use sparingly)
- **Overlays**: Graphics, timers, icons

### When to Use Effects
- Enhance story, not distract
- Match brand/style
- Less is often more
- Consistent throughout video

## Workflow Efficiency

### Keyboard Shortcuts (Essential)
- **Play/Pause**: Space or K
- **Cut**: Cmd/Ctrl + K or Blade tool
- **Ripple delete**: Shift + Delete
- **Zoom timeline**: + / -
- **Mark in/out**: I / O

### Time-Savers
- **Presets**: Save favorite effects
- **Proxy files**: Edit lower-res, export full-res
- **Batch processing**: Apply changes to multiple clips
- **Markers**: Flag important moments
- **Bins/folders**: Organize clips

### Hardware Optimization
- **RAM**: 16GB minimum, 32GB+ ideal
- **GPU**: NVIDIA/AMD for rendering
- **Storage**: SSD for working files, HDD for archives
- **Monitor**: Color-accurate for grading

### Feedback Management
- **Timecoded notes**: Specific timestamps
- **Version control**: Track changes (_v01, _v02)
- **Frame.io / Wipster**: Online review platforms
- **Set revision limits**: 2-3 rounds standard

### Delivering Files
- **Master file**: Highest quality archive
- **Deliverable**: Platform-optimized export

## Common Challenges

- **Slow exports**: Use proxies, optimize settings, hardware limitations
- **Out of sync audio**: Lock audio/video, check sample rate
- **Footage mismatch**: Color correct, adjust frame rates
- **Too much footage**: Be ruthless in cutting
- **Unclear direction**: Get detailed brief upfront

## Best Practices

### Technical
- **Save often**: Auto-save every 5-10 min
- **Backup projects**: Multiple locations
- **Organize files**: Consistent naming and structure
- **Render previews**: Smooth playback
- **Check audio levels**: No clipping
- **Export settings**: Match platform specs

### Creative
- **Tell a story**: Every cut should have purpose
- **Pace for retention**: Vary rhythm
- **Cut on motion**: Smoother transitions
- **Kill your darlings**: Remove unnecessary shots
- **Show, don't tell**: Visual > explanatory
- **Match audio to visuals**: Sync mood and energy
- **Test on mobile**: Most viewers watch on phones

## Export Settings (General)

### YouTube/Vimeo
- **Format**: MP4 (H.264)
- **Resolution**: 1080p or 4K
- **Frame rate**: Match source (23.98, 24, 30, 60)
- **Bitrate**: 8-12 Mbps (1080p), 35-45 Mbps (4K)
- **Audio**: AAC, 320 kbps, 48kHz

### Social Media
- **Instagram/TikTok**: MP4, 1080x1920, max 30 Mbps
- **Facebook**: MP4, various aspect ratios, max 50 Mbps
