## Key Federal Environmental Laws (US)

### 1. Clean Air Act (CAA)
- **Purpose**: Regulate air emissions from stationary and mobile sources
- **Key Programs**: National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS), National Emissions Standards for Hazardous Air Pollutants (NESHAP), acid rain program, ozone protection
- **Compliance**: Permits (Title V), monitoring, recordkeeping, reporting
- **Impact**: Affects manufacturing, power generation, transportation

### 2. Clean Water Act (CWA)
- **Purpose**: Restore and maintain chemical, physical, and biological integrity of nation's waters
- **Key Programs**: National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) permits for point sources, water quality standards, nonpoint source management
- **Compliance**: Prohibits discharge of pollutants without a permit, monitoring, reporting
- **Impact**: Affects industries discharging wastewater, construction, agriculture

### 3. RCRA (Resource Conservation and Recovery Act)
- **Purpose**: Regulate management of solid waste and hazardous waste from cradle to grave
- **Key Programs**: Hazardous waste identification, manifesting, treatment, storage, and disposal (TSD) facility permits
- **Compliance**: Waste generator requirements, transporter rules, TSD facility standards, underground storage tank (UST) regulations
- **Impact**: Affects all businesses generating waste, especially hazardous waste

### 4. CERCLA (Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act - Superfund)
- **Purpose**: Address releases of hazardous substances and remediate contaminated sites
- **Key Programs**: National Priorities List (NPL) for most serious sites, liability scheme for responsible parties
- **Liability**: Strict, joint, several, and retroactive (can be liable for past contamination)
- **Impact**: Affects property owners, operators, generators, transporters of hazardous substances, M&A due diligence

### 5. NEPA (National Environmental Policy Act)
- **Purpose**: Ensure federal agencies consider environmental impacts of their actions
- **Key Process**: Environmental Assessment (EA) to determine if significant impact; Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) if significant impact
- **Public involvement**: Requires public review and comment
- **Impact**: Applies to federal projects, projects requiring federal permits or funding

### 6. Endangered Species Act (ESA)
- **Purpose**: Protect endangered and threatened species and their habitats
- **Key Prohibitions**: "Take" of listed species (harass, harm, pursue, hunt, shoot, wound, kill, trap, capture, collect)
- **Critical habitat**: Designation and protection of essential areas
- **Impact**: Affects land development, resource extraction, federal projects

## Environmental Permitting & Compliance

### Permits
- **Air permits**: For emissions sources
- **Water permits**: For wastewater discharge, storm water runoff
- **Waste permits**: For hazardous waste storage/treatment
- **Purpose**: Limit pollution, ensure safe operations
- **Process**: Application, review, public notice, issuance, renewal

### Compliance Obligations
- **Monitoring**: Stack testing, water sampling, waste analysis
- **Recordkeeping**: Maintain permits, test results, training logs
- **Reporting**: Annual reports, deviation reports, incident reports
- **Training**: Employee training on environmental procedures
- **Inspections**: Regular self-inspections and agency inspections

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)

### Process
1. **Screening**: Determine if EIA is needed
2. **Scoping**: Define scope of assessment
3. **Baseline data collection**: Current environmental conditions
4. **Impact prediction**: Analyze potential effects
5. **Mitigation**: Propose measures to reduce impacts
6. **Public participation**: Solicit feedback
7. **Decision making**: Integrate EIA into project decision
8. **Monitoring**: Track impacts post-project

### US Actions

## Due Diligence & Audits

### Environmental Due Diligence (M&A, Real Estate)
- **Phase I ESA**: Historical review, site visit (ASTM Standard)
- **Phase II ESA**: Sampling (soil, groundwater) if Phase I finds concerns
- **Purpose**: Identify environmental liabilities, assess risk

### Environmental Compliance Audits
- **Purpose**: Verify compliance with regulations and permits
- **Scope**: Air, water, waste, chemicals, management systems
- **Output**: Identify non-compliance, recommend corrective actions

## Enforcement & Penalties

### Violations Can Lead To:
- **Administrative orders**: EPA/state orders to comply
- **Civil penalties**: Fines (can be very high)
- **Criminal penalties**: For knowing violations (imprisonment)
- **Citizen suits**: Individuals/groups can sue violators
- **Supplemental Environmental Projects (SEPs)**: Agreed-upon projects to offset penalties

## Environmental Management Systems (EMS)

### ISO 14001
- **Standard**: International standard for EMS
- **Purpose**: Systematize environmental management, improve performance
- **Process**: Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA)
- **Benefits**: Improved compliance, reduced waste, enhanced reputation

### Concepts
