# Estate Planning Basics

Estate planning guidance specialist helping individuals understand wills, trusts, powers of attorney, and end-of-life planning.

## Core Documents
### Last Will and Testament
**Purpose**: Directs distribution of assets after death
**Key provisions**:
- Executor appointment
- Asset distribution
- Guardian for minor children
- Specific bequests
- Residuary clause (everything else)

**What it does NOT cover**:
- Assets with beneficiary designations
- Jointly owned property
- Assets in trust
- Life insurance proceeds (go to beneficiaries)

### Living Trust (Revocable Trust)
**Purpose**: Manages assets during life and distributes after death
**Benefits**:
- Avoids probate (faster, private)
- Maintains privacy (not public record)
- Easier to manage if incapacitated
- Can control distribution timing

**Types**:
- **Revocable**: Can change or revoke
- **Irrevocable**: Cannot change (tax benefits, asset protection)

**Downsides**:
- Setup cost ($1k-$3k+)
- Must fund trust (transfer assets)
- Complexity

### Power of Attorney (POA)
**Purpose**: Grants authority to make decisions on your behalf
**Types**:
- **Financial POA**: Handle finances, pay bills, manage property
- **Healthcare POA**: Make medical decisions
- **Durable**: Remains in effect if incapacitated
- **Springing**: Only active upon specific event

**Important**: Terminates at death (then executor takes over)

### Healthcare Directive (Living Will)
**Purpose**: Specifies end-of-life medical wishes
**Covers**:
- Life-sustaining treatment preferences
- DNR (Do Not Resuscitate) orders
- Organ donation wishes
- Pain management
- Specific medical scenarios

### Beneficiary Designations
**Applies to**:
- Retirement accounts (401k, IRA)
- Life insurance
- Bank accounts (POD - Payable on Death)
- Investment accounts (TOD - Transfer on Death)

**Important**: These override your will!
**Update regularly**: After marriage, divorce, births, deaths

## When You Need Estate Planning
### Everyone Should Have
- Basic will
- Healthcare directive
- Healthcare POA
- Financial POA (durable)

### You Especially Need Planning If...
- You have minor children (guardian designation)
- You own real estate
- You have significant assets ($100k+)
- You own a business
- You have a blended family
- You want to minimize taxes
- You want to avoid probate
- You have specific wishes

## Probate
### What Is Probate?
- Court process to validate will
- Settle debts
- Distribute assets
- Public record
- Can take 6-18 months
- Costs 3-7% of estate

### Avoiding Probate
- Living trust (most common)
- Joint ownership with right of survivorship
- Beneficiary designations
- POD/TOD accounts
- Small estate procedures (if under state threshold)

### When Probate Is Okay
- Simple estate
- No disputes expected
- Assets below threshold
- Cost not a concern
- Privacy not important

## Guardianship for Minor Children
### Naming a Guardian
- **Guardian of person**: Raise children
- **Guardian of property**: Manage inheritance
- Can be same or different people

### Considerations
- Willingness and ability
- Age and health
- Values and parenting style
- Location
- Financial stability
- Name alternates

## Estate Tax
### Federal Estate Tax
- **Exemption (2024)**: $13.61 million per person
- **Portability**: Spouses can combine ($27.22M)
- **Only ultra-wealthy affected**: <0.1% of estates
- **Rate**: 40% on amount over exemption

### State Estate/Inheritance Tax
- About 12 states have estate tax
- Lower exemptions ($1M-$5M typically)
- Inheritance tax (tax on beneficiaries in some states)
- Check your state laws

### Tax Planning Strategies (If Applicable)
- Lifetime gifting ($18,000/person/year, 2024)
- Irrevocable life insurance trusts
- Charitable donations
- Family limited partnerships
- Qualified Personal Residence Trust (QPRT)

## Special Considerations
### Blended Families
- Potential conflicts between spouse and children
- Use of trusts to protect all parties
- Clear communication critical
- Consider life insurance

### Business Owners
- Succession planning
- Buy-sell agreements
- Business valuation
- Key person insurance
- Transition timeline

### Digital Assets
- Social media accounts
- Email and cloud storage
- Cryptocurrency wallets
- Online accounts and subscriptions
- Digital photos and files
- Provide access instructions

## Updating Your Estate Plan
### When to Review
- Marriage or divorce
- Birth or adoption of children
- Death of beneficiary or executor
- Significant change in assets
- Move to different state
- Change in tax laws
- Every 3-5 years (general review)

### What to Update
- Beneficiary designations
- Executor/trustee appointments
- Guardian designations
- Asset changes
- Wishes and preferences

## Common Mistakes
- No estate plan at all
- Outdated beneficiary designations
- Not funding a trust
- Forgetting digital assets
- Not discussing with family
- Choosing wrong executor
- DIY without attorney review (for complex estates)
- Procrastinating

## DIY vs Attorney
### When DIY Might Be Okay
- Simple estate
- Standard wishes
- No unusual circumstances
- Low assets
- No blended family
- Use quality software (Trust & Will, LegalZoom)

### When to Hire Attorney
- Significant assets ($500k+)
- Blended family
- Own business
- Complex family dynamics
- Estate tax concerns
- Special needs beneficiaries
- Want personalized guidance

### Cost
- **Simple will**: $300-$1,000
- **Living trust package**: $1,500-$3,500
- **Complex estate plan**: $5,000-$10,000+

## Estate Planning for Different Life Stages
### 20s-30s (Starting Out)
- Basic will
- Beneficiary designations
- Healthcare directive
- Guardian for children (if applicable)

### 40s-50s (Building Wealth)
- Living trust (consider)
- Review beneficiaries
- Life insurance review
- Update as assets grow

### 60s+ (Pre-Retirement)
- Comprehensive estate plan
- Tax planning
- Long-term care planning
- Finalize executor/trustee choices

### Retirement
- Minimize probate
- Ensure beneficiaries updated
- Consider gifting strategies
- Plan required minimum distributions (RMDs)

## End-of-Life Planning
### Funeral and Burial
- Pre-plan and pre-pay (optional)
- Communicate wishes
- Cemetery plot or cremation
- Funeral service preferences
- Obituary information

### Final Arrangements Checklist
- Location of important documents
- Account numbers and passwords
- Insurance policies
- Debt information
- Professional contacts (lawyer, CPA, advisor)
- Funeral wishes
- Obituary draft

## Finding an Estate Planning Attorney
- **Referrals**: Friends, family, financial advisor
- **State bar association**: Lawyer referral service
- **Specialization**: Board-certified estate planning attorney
- **Questions to ask**: Experience, fees, process, timeline
