## Types of Intellectual Property

### 1. Patents
- **Protects**: Inventions (new, useful, non-obvious processes, machines, articles of manufacture, compositions of matter)
- **Duration**: 20 years from filing date (utility patent)
- **Requirements**: Novelty, utility, non-obviousness
- **Process**: Provisional application (optional), Non-provisional application, Examination, Grant
- **Rights**: Excludes others from making, using, selling invention

### 2. Copyrights
- **Protects**: Original works of authorship fixed in a tangible medium (literary, musical, dramatic, pictorial, graphic, sculptural, motion pictures, sound recordings, architectural, software code)
- **Duration**: Life of author + 70 years (individual); 95 years from publication or 120 years from creation (corporate)
- **Requirements**: Originality, fixation
- **Process**: Automatic upon creation; registration with Copyright Office for stronger enforcement
- **Rights**: Reproduce, distribute, perform, display, create derivative works

### 3. Trademarks
- **Protects**: Brand names, logos, slogans, symbols used to identify goods/services and distinguish them from others
- **Duration**: Indefinite if used and renewed
- **Requirements**: Distinctiveness, use in commerce
- **Process**: Common law rights (local use) vs. federal registration (USPTO)
- **Rights**: Prevents others from using confusingly similar marks
- **™**: Unregistered trademark; **®**: Federally registered trademark

### 4. Trade Secrets
- **Protects**: Confidential business information that provides a competitive advantage (formulas, practices, designs, instruments, patterns, commercial methods, compilations of information)
- **Duration**: Indefinite, as long as kept secret
- **Requirements**: Must be secret, provide economic value due to secrecy, reasonable efforts to maintain secrecy
- **Process**: No registration; protection by maintaining confidentiality
- **Rights**: Prevents misappropriation (theft)

## IP Strategy & Protection

### Before Public Disclosure
- **File patents** (if applicable)
- **Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs)**: Use with partners, employees, contractors
- **Document everything**: Invention disclosures, creative process, dates

### Patent Strategy
- **Search**: Prior art search to assess patentability
- **Provisional patent**: Establishes early filing date (1 year to file non-provisional)
- **Strategic filing**: Domestic vs. international (PCT)
- **Maintenance fees**: Pay periodically to keep active

### Copyright Strategy
- **Copyright notice**: © [Year] [Name]
- **Registration**: Register with Copyright Office for statutory damages, attorney's fees
- **Licensing**: Grant permission for use
- **Work for hire**: Ensure contracts clarify ownership

### Trademark Strategy
- **Clearance search**: Ensure mark is available and distinctive
- **Registration**: Federal registration (USPTO) provides nationwide rights, public notice, ability to sue in federal court
- **Monitoring**: Watch for infringing uses
- **Use it or lose it**: Must use mark in commerce to maintain rights

### Trade Secret Strategy
- **Restrict access**: Limit who knows
- **Confidentiality agreements**: NDAs with employees, partners
- **Physical & digital security**: Lock it up
- **Mark as confidential**: Clearly label documents
- **Employee training**: Educate on protecting secrets

## IP Agreements & Licensing

### Licensing Agreements
- **Grant of rights**: What can licensee do?
- **Scope**: Exclusive vs. non-exclusive, territory, duration, field of use
- **Royalties**: Payment structure
- **Quality control**: Licensor oversight
- **Termination**: How agreement can end

### IP Assignments
- **Transfer of ownership**: "All right, title, and interest"
- **Employees**: Often assign IP created within scope of employment
- **Contractors**: Ensure explicit IP assignment clauses

## IP Infringement & Enforcement

### What is Infringement?
- **Patent**: Unauthorized making, using, selling of patented invention
- **Copyright**: Unauthorized reproduction, distribution, display, performance, derivative work
- **Trademark**: Use of confusingly similar mark for goods/services
- **Trade Secret**: Misappropriation (theft, improper acquisition)

### Enforcement Options
- **Cease and desist letter**: Formal notice of infringement
- **Negotiation/settlement**: Resolve without litigation
- **Litigation**: Lawsuit for damages, injunction
- **DMCA takedown**: For online copyright infringement
- **Customs & Border Protection**: Prevent import of infringing goods

### Defenses to Infringement
- **Invalidity**: Patent/copyright/trademark is not valid
- **Non-infringement**: Actions don't meet legal definition
- **Fair use**: For copyright (parody, criticism, education)
- **First sale doctrine**: Resell lawfully purchased items
- **Prior use**: For trademarks

## Open Source Software (OSS) Considerations

### License Compliance
- **Understand licenses**: GPL, MIT, Apache, etc.
- **Obligations**: Attribution, share-alike, notice requirements
- **Compliance audits**: Ensure proper use of OSS
- **Avoid license contamination**: "Copyleft" licenses can affect proprietary code

## Key Takeaways
- **Identify your IP**: Know what you have
- **Protect your IP**: Use appropriate legal tools
- **Document everything**: Proof of creation, efforts to keep secret
- **Act early**: Filing dates are crucial for patents
- **Monitor for infringement**: Watch for unauthorized use
- **Consult attorney**: Complex area, state-specific nuances
