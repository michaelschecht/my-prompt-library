## Retirement Accounts
### 401(k) / 403(b) (Employer Plans)
- **Contribution limit (2024)**: $23,000
- **Catch-up (50+)**: Additional $7,500
- **Tax treatment**: Traditional (pre-tax) or Roth (post-tax)
- **Employer match**: Free money—always contribute enough to get full match
- **Vesting**: Check employer match vesting schedule

### IRA (Individual Retirement Account)
- **Contribution limit**: $7,000
- **Catch-up (50+)**: Additional $1,000
- **Traditional IRA**: Pre-tax contributions, taxed in retirement
- **Roth IRA**: Post-tax contributions, tax-free in retirement
- **Income limits**: Roth has income phase-outs

### Roth vs Traditional Decision
**Choose Roth if:**
- Lower tax bracket now than in retirement
- Young with decades to grow tax-free
- Want tax diversification
- Expect higher future tax rates

**Choose Traditional if:**
- Higher tax bracket now
- Near retirement
- Want immediate tax deduction
- Lower income expected in retirement

### Other Accounts
- **HSA** (Health Savings Account): Triple tax advantage, underrated retirement tool
- **SEP IRA**: Self-employed, up to 25% of income
- **Solo 401(k)**: Self-employed, higher limits
- **457(b)**: Government employees, no early withdrawal penalty

## How Much to Save
### General Guidelines
- **15% of gross income** for retirement
- **Start early**: Compound interest is powerful
- **Increase with raises**: Lifestyle inflation is silent killer

### Retirement Number Estimation
**25x Rule (4% withdrawal rate):**
- Annual expenses × 25 = retirement savings needed
- Example: $50,000/year expenses = $1.25M needed
- Based on 4% safe withdrawal rate

**Retirement Calculator Inputs:**
- Current age and retirement age
- Current savings
- Annual contributions
- Expected return (7-8% historical average)
- Life expectancy (plan to 95+)

## Investment Strategy
### Asset Allocation by Age
**Aggressive (20s-30s):**
- 90% stocks, 10% bonds
- Higher growth potential
- Can weather volatility

**Moderate (40s-50s):**
- 70-80% stocks, 20-30% bonds
- Balanced growth and stability

**Conservative (60s+):**
- 50-60% stocks, 40-50% bonds
- Preserve wealth, reduce volatility
- Rule of thumb: (110 - age) = % in stocks

### Target-Date Funds
- Automatic rebalancing
- Age-appropriate allocation
- "Set and forget" option
- Check expense ratios (0.1-0.5% good)

### Index Fund Approach
- Low-cost index funds (Vanguard, Fidelity, Schwab)
- Total stock market
- International stocks
- Bond index
- Rebalance annually

## Social Security
### When to Claim
- **62 (earliest)**: Reduced benefits (25-30%)
- **Full Retirement Age (66-67)**: Full benefit
- **70 (latest)**: Maximum benefit (+24-32%)
- **Each year delay**: +8% benefit (from FRA to 70)

### Optimization Strategies
- Higher earner delays to 70
- Lower earner claims earlier
- Spousal benefits
- Survivor benefits
- Work history (35 highest-earning years)

## Withdrawal Strategies
### 4% Rule
- Withdraw 4% of portfolio year 1
- Adjust for inflation each year
- Historically safe for 30-year retirement
- Conservative approach

### Bucket Strategy
- Bucket 1: 1-2 years cash (safety)
- Bucket 2: 3-10 years bonds (stability)
- Bucket 3: 10+ years stocks (growth)
- Refill buckets from gains

### Tax-Efficient Withdrawal Order
1. RMDs (Required Minimum Distributions) first
2. Taxable accounts (brokerage)
3. Tax-deferred (traditional IRA, 401k)
4. Tax-free (Roth IRA) last

## Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)
- **Starts**: Age 73 (as of 2023 law)
- **Accounts**: Traditional IRA, 401(k), 403(b)
- **Not required**: Roth IRA, Roth 401(k)
- **Penalty**: 25% of amount not withdrawn
- **Calculate annually**: IRS tables

## Healthcare in Retirement
### Medicare (65+)
- **Part A**: Hospital insurance (usually free)
- **Part B**: Medical insurance (premium)
- **Part D**: Prescription drugs
- **Medigap**: Supplemental insurance
- **Enroll on time**: Penalties for late enrollment

### Before Medicare (retire before 65)
- COBRA (expensive, 18-36 months)
- ACA marketplace
- Spouse's insurance
- Part-time job with benefits
- HSA savings help

## Common Mistakes
- Not starting early enough
- Not getting employer match (free money!)
- Cashing out 401(k) when changing jobs
- Too conservative when young
- Too aggressive when old
- Ignoring fees (1% fee = huge impact)
- Not planning for healthcare costs
- Underestimating life expectancy

## Catch-Up Strategies (Behind on Savings)
- Max out catch-up contributions (50+)
- Delay retirement a few years
- Reduce retirement expenses
- Part-time work in retirement
- Downsize home
- Relocate to lower COL area
- Pay off debt before retirement

## Key Milestones
- **20s**: Start contributing, get match
- **30s**: Increase savings rate, invest aggressively
- **40s**: Check progress, aim for 3x salary saved
- **50s**: Max contributions, catch-up if behind
- **60s**: Shift to preservation, plan withdrawal strategy
- **70s**: RMDs begin, optimize Social Security

*Start early, save consistently, invest wisely.* 🏖️
