# AI Agent Prompts for Online Course Niche Analysis

This sequence of prompts guides an AI agent in analyzing market trends to identify underserved niches for new online courses.

## 1. Market Research: Identifying Trends

**Prompt:**
"Analyze the current trends in the online education market. Identify the top 5 fastest-growing subject areas. For each area, provide data on market size, growth projections, and key driving factors. Use recent industry reports and market analysis from reputable sources."

## 2. Competitor Analysis: Mapping the Landscape

**Prompt:**
"For each of the top 5 subject areas identified, conduct a competitive analysis of the existing online course landscape. Identify the top 3-5 major platforms or providers (e.g., Coursera, Udemy, LinkedIn Learning, independent creators) and their most popular courses. Analyze their offerings, pricing, and target audience."

## 3. Audience Insights: Uncovering Unmet Needs

**Prompt:**
"Analyze online communities, forums (e.g., Reddit, Quora), and social media discussions related to the top 5 subject areas. Identify common questions, pain points, and unmet needs of learners. What are people struggling with that existing courses don't adequately address?"

## 4. Niche Identification: Brainstorming & Evaluation

**Prompt:**
"Based on the market trends, competitor landscape, and audience insights, brainstorm a list of 10 potential underserved niches for new online courses. For each niche, provide a brief description and a rationale for why it is underserved. Evaluate each niche against the following criteria: potential audience size, learner urgency, and competition level."

## 5. Validation: Gauging Demand

**Prompt:**
"For the top 3 most promising niches from the previous step, propose a strategy to validate market demand. This should include at least three different methods, such as a content-based demand test (e.g., a blog post or YouTube video), a pre-sale offer, and a survey to a targeted audience. Outline the key metrics to track for each validation method."
