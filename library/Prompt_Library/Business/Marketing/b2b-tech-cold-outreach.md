# B2B Tech Cold Outreach Campaign

## Purpose
Create a high-converting, multi-touch cold email outreach sequence designed to generate qualified sales meetings for a B2B technology or service company.

## Instructions
Act as a seasoned B2B sales development representative (SDR). Write a 4-step cold email sequence targeting decision-makers (e.g., CTOs, VP of Engineering, IT Directors) at mid-market to enterprise companies. The sequence must avoid generic marketing speak, focus heavily on the prospect's pain points, be highly personalized, and include soft, frictionless calls-to-action (CTAs).

Define the specific technology or service being sold (e.g., custom software development, cloud migration services, enterprise data analytics), the target persona, and the primary pain point being solved.

## Output Format
Outline the outreach campaign as follows:
1. **Targeting & Persona Profile**: Define the ideal customer profile (ICP), target titles, and the specific triggers indicating they might need your service.
2. **Email 1: The Personalized Hook (Day 1)**: Focus on relevance, brief value proposition, and a soft CTA (e.g., "Open to learning more?").
3. **Email 2: The Value Add/Resource (Day 4)**: Following up with a relevant case study, industry report, or actionable insight.
4. **Email 3: The Agitation/Pain Point (Day 8)**: Highlighting the cost of inaction or a common frustration.
5. **Email 4: The Breakup/Permission to Close (Day 14)**: A professional, low-pressure final attempt asking if the timing is wrong.

## Example
**Input:**
Service: Cloud migration and infrastructure modernization consulting.
Target Persona: CTOs or VP of Infrastructure at mid-sized e-commerce companies.
Pain Point: High AWS costs and slow site performance during traffic spikes.

**Output:**
### 1. Targeting & Persona Profile
**ICP:** E-commerce companies doing $10M-$50M annually.
**Titles:** CTO, VP of Engineering, Head of Infrastructure.
**Trigger:** Recently announced a major sale event or experiencing rapid growth.

### Email 1: The Personalized Hook (Day 1)
**Subject:** Question about [Company Name]'s infrastructure scaling
**Body:**
Hi [Name],
Noticed [Company Name] is gearing up for your upcoming [Sale Event/Season].

Many e-commerce engineering teams we speak with find that their AWS costs spike unpredictably during these high-traffic periods, while site latency still suffers.

We recently helped [Competitor/Similar Company] modernize their cloud architecture, resulting in a 30% reduction in monthly cloud spend and zero downtime during Black Friday.

Are you open to a brief chat next week to see how we achieved this?

Best,
[Signature]

*(Continues for the remaining 3 emails)*
