# Tech Agency Re-engagement Campaign

## Purpose
Create a "win-back" or re-engagement email sequence to revive dormant relationships with past clients or unconverted leads for a specialized technology agency (e.g., web development, UX/UI design, SEO).

## Instructions
Act as an agency founder or director of business development. Write a 3-part email sequence aimed at past clients who haven't engaged in over 6-12 months, or warm leads who "went dark" after an initial proposal. The tone should be highly personal, non-needy, and focused on providing new value or sharing an exciting update rather than just asking for work.

Define the agency's specialty, the target audience (past clients vs. ghosted leads), and the specific "excuse" for reaching out (e.g., new capability, industry shift, recent award).

## Output Format
Outline the re-engagement campaign as follows:
1. **Campaign Strategy**: The target list criteria and the primary goal (e.g., booking a catch-up call).
2. **Email 1: The Value-Led Update (Day 1)**: Reconnecting by sharing something genuinely useful (a trend, a new capability, or a relevant piece of content).
3. **Email 2: The Direct "Checking In" (Day 7)**: A shorter, more direct email referencing their past project or goals.
4. **Email 3: The "Closing the Loop" (Day 14)**: The final, low-friction email known as the 9-word email or a professional sign-off.

## Example
**Input:**
Agency Specialty: Enterprise Mobile App Development.
Target Audience: Past clients (projects completed 12+ months ago).
Excuse to reach out: Launch of a new UI modernization sprint service.

**Output:**
### 1. Campaign Strategy
**Target List:** Former clients with apps deployed in 2022 or earlier.
**Goal:** Book a 15-minute "app health check" call.

### Email 1: The Value-Led Update (Day 1)
**Subject:** Quick update from [Agency Name] / Mobile UX trends for 2024
**Body:**
Hi [Name],
It's been a while since we wrapped up the [Project Name] app! I hope you and the team at [Company] are doing well.

I'm reaching out because Apple and Google have introduced some major UI changes recently that are impacting conversion rates on older apps. We just published a brief guide on modernizing legacy app interfaces, and given our past work, I thought you'd find it relevant: [Link to Guide].

We're also now offering 2-week UI Modernization Sprints specifically for our past clients.

Let me know if you’d like to grab 15 minutes next week to see if a quick refresh makes sense for your current user base.

Best,
[Signature]

*(Continues for Emails 2 and 3)*
