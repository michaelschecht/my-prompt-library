## Sales Deck Structure (10-12 slides)

### 1. Title Slide
- Product/service name and tagline
- Your name and title
- Company logo
- Date and contact info

### 2. About Us (Optional - keep brief)
- Who we are (1-2 sentences)
- Notable customers or achievements
- Industry credibility signals
- **Keep it short** - they care about their problem, not your history

### 3. Understanding Your Challenge
- Show you understand **their** specific pain point
- Use their industry language
- Reference their situation (research beforehand)
- Build empathy and credibility

### 4. The Cost of Inaction
- What happens if they don't solve this?
- Quantify the impact (time, money, opportunity cost)
- Create urgency
- Use their numbers if possible

### 5. Our Solution
- How we solve their specific problem
- Key features that address their pain points
- Simple explanation, not technical jargon
- Show the product (demo, screenshots, video)

### 6. How It Works
- Implementation process (simple 3-5 steps)
- Timeline to value
- Integration with their existing systems
- What they need to provide vs. what you handle

### 7. Value & ROI
- Quantified benefits (time saved, revenue gained, costs reduced)
- ROI calculation with their numbers
- Payback period
- Comparison: cost vs. value delivered

### 8. Social Proof
- Customer testimonials (similar companies/industries)
- Case studies with metrics
- Logos of recognizable customers
- Industry awards or certifications

### 9. Why Us vs. Competition
- Competitive differentiation
- Why customers choose you
- Unique features or approach
- What makes you better for **their** specific need

### 10. Pricing & Packages
- Clear pricing tiers or options
- What's included in each
- Recommended package for them (guided choice)
- Optional add-ons
- Any current promotions or incentives

### 11. Implementation & Support
- Onboarding process
- Training provided
- Ongoing support options
- Resources available (documentation, community, etc.)

### 12. Next Steps
- Clear call-to-action
- Timeline for decision and implementation
- What happens after "yes"
- Trial or pilot option if applicable
- Contact information

## Tips & Best Practices

### Do's
- **Research first:** Know their business and challenges
- **Customize:** Tailor deck to their industry/situation
- **Show, don't tell:** Demo > features list
- **Use their numbers:** ROI with their actual data
- **Build trust:** Social proof from similar companies
- **Make it easy:** Clear next steps, remove friction
- **Listen more than talk:** Ask questions, understand needs
- **Follow up:** Send deck + summary within 24 hours

### Don'ts
- **Don't lead with you:** Start with their problem
- **Don't overwhelm:** Too many features confuses
- **Don't ignore competition:** Address it proactively
- **Don't overpromise:** Realistic expectations win trust
- **Don't forget ROI:** Executives care about numbers
- **Don't end without CTA:** Always have clear next step
- **Don't pitch without qualifying:** Know they're a fit first

## Sales Conversation Framework

### Discovery Phase (Before Deck)
**Key Questions:**
- What's your current process for [problem area]?
- What challenges are you facing?
- What have you tried before?
- Who else is involved in this decision?
- What's your timeline?
- What would success look like?

### Presentation Phase (With Deck)
- **Start with their pain** (not your solution)
- **Demo real use case** (their scenario)
- **Handle objections** (have backup slides)
- **Check for engagement** (pause, ask questions)
- **Summarize value** (their ROI, not general)

### Closing Phase (After Deck)
- **Ask for the sale:** "Does this solve [their problem]?"
- **Address concerns:** "What questions do you have?"
- **Define next steps:** Trial, contract, implementation
- **Set timeline:** When can we start?
- **Get commitment:** Even small yes moves it forward

## Objection Handling

### "Too expensive"
- Show ROI calculation
- Offer payment options
- Highlight cost of inaction
- Pilot program or trial

### "Need to think about it"
- Clarify specific concerns
- Offer to address with stakeholders
- Set follow-up meeting
- Share resources for internal championing

### "We're using [competitor]"
- Acknowledge and respect their choice
- Highlight your differentiators
- Offer comparison or trial
- Migration support if switching

### "Not the right time"
- Understand timeline constraints
- Cost of waiting (quantified)
- Phased implementation option
- Stay in touch strategy

## Industry-Specific Customization

### Enterprise/B2B
- Emphasize security, compliance, scale
- Include executive summary slide
- Procurement-friendly pricing
- Integration and SSO capabilities

### Small Business/SMB
- Simple setup, minimal IT needed
- Affordable pricing, no hidden costs
- Quick time to value
- Self-service options

### Technical Audience
- Include architecture slide
- API documentation reference
- Security certifications
- Technical differentiators

### Executive Audience
- Lead with business impact
- Strategic value, not features
- Competitive advantage
- Risk mitigation
