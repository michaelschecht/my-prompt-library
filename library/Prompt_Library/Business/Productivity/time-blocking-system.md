# Time Blocking System Designer

## Purpose
Creates a personalized time blocking schedule that maximizes deep work, minimizes context switching, and aligns daily activities with long-term goals. Based on Cal Newport's Deep Work methodology and energy management principles.

## Instructions

I want you to act as a productivity coach specializing in time blocking. Help me design an optimal daily/weekly time blocking system.

**Current Situation:**
- **Work type:** [e.g., software engineer, writer, manager, entrepreneur]
- **Work hours:** [e.g., 9-5, flexible, shift work]
- **Energy peaks:** [When are you most focused? Morning/afternoon/evening]
- **Current challenges:** [Interruptions, procrastination, overcommitment, etc.]
- **Goals:** [What do you want to achieve? Projects, habits, learning]

**Create a time blocking system that includes:**

### 1. Deep Work Blocks
- Schedule 2-4 hour blocks for cognitively demanding work
- Place during energy peak times
- Eliminate distractions (no meetings, email, Slack)
- Specify what types of tasks go here

### 2. Shallow Work Blocks
- Email, admin, meetings, logistics
- Group similar tasks together (batch processing)
- Place during lower-energy periods

### 3. Buffer Blocks
- Catch-up time for overflow tasks
- Unexpected urgent items
- Typically 30-60 min between major blocks

### 4. Recovery Blocks
- Breaks, lunch, exercise, walks
- Essential for sustained performance
- Minimum 15 min every 90 min of focused work

### 5. Theme Days (Optional)
- Monday: Strategy & Planning
- Tuesday: Creation/Building
- Wednesday: Meetings & Collaboration
- Thursday: Execution/Implementation  
- Friday: Review & Admin

**Output Format:**
- Visual daily schedule (time blocks)
- Weekly template
- Rules for protecting time blocks
- Meeting scheduling guidelines
- Emergency handling procedures

```markdown
## Time Block Protection Rules

### Protecting Deep Work Blocks

1. **No meetings during deep work** (7-9 AM, 9:30 AM-12 PM)
   - Exception: CEO/critical stakeholder emergency only
   - Reschedule recurring meetings to collaborative blocks

2. **Communication blackout**
   - Email: Closed (auto-responder: "In deep work, will respond 1-2 PM")
   - Slack: Snoozed notifications, status "🔴 Focus mode"
   - Phone: DND except favorites

3. **Environmental setup**
   - Headphones on (even if no music)
   - Door closed or "busy" sign
   - Webcam covered

4. **Pre-commitment**
   - Block on calendar as "Busy" (not "Free")
   - Title: "🔵 Deep Work - Decline meetings"
   - Recurring weekly blocks

### Meeting Scheduling Guidelines

**When you can take meetings:**
- Monday: 3-5 PM
- Tuesday: 3-5 PM
- Wednesday: 1-5 PM (collaboration day)
- Thursday: 3-5 PM
- Friday: 9 AM-12 PM, 3-5 PM

**Default responses:**
- "My mornings are blocked for focused work. Can we do [afternoon time]?"
- "I batch meetings Wed/Fri. Would [specific time] work?"
- "I'm protecting my deep work time. Let's find a slot in my collaboration blocks."

**Red lines:**
- No meetings before 1 PM (except Fridays)
- No meetings longer than 60 min without clear agenda
- Decline recurring meetings with >5 people (async instead)

## Emergency Handling

### If urgent issue arises during deep work:

**Triage Decision Tree:**

1. **Is it truly urgent?** (Will waiting 2 hours cause real harm?)
   - ✅ Yes: Handle now (but set 30 min timer)
   - ❌ No: Add to buffer block list

2. **Can someone else handle it?**
   - ✅ Yes: Delegate immediately
   - ❌ No: Proceed to step 3

3. **Will it take <5 minutes?**
   - ✅ Yes: Handle now, return to deep work
   - ❌ No: Move to buffer block, reschedule current task

**Recovery after interruption:**
- Take 5 min walk to reset
- Review what you were doing (check notes)
- Set timer for remaining deep work time
- Resume with fresh focus

## Buffer Block Strategy

**What goes in buffer blocks (2:30-3 PM daily):**
- Overflow from deep work that ran long
- Urgent items from Slack/email
- Quick calls that came up
- Prep/research for tomorrow's deep work

**Never scheduled in advance** - intentionally kept flexible

## Metrics to Track (Weekly Review)

**Deep work effectiveness:**
- [ ] Did I complete 2+ hours of uninterrupted deep work daily?
- [ ] What % of planned deep work tasks were completed?
- [ ] How many interruptions occurred during deep work blocks?
- [ ] Did I protect my time blocks (yes/no each day)?

**Energy alignment:**
- [ ] Did I tackle hardest tasks during peak energy?
- [ ] Did I take adequate breaks?
- [ ] Did I end work at planned time (prevent burnout)?

**Weekly progress:**
- [ ] Did I move the needle on my most important project?
- [ ] What would I do differently next week?

## Customization Options

### For Shift Workers
- Identify your peak energy window (even if it's 2 PM or 8 PM)
- Place deep work blocks there
- Adapt template to your shift schedule

### For Parents
- Deep work blocks when kids are at school/daycare
- Shallow work during nap times
- Accept imperfect weeks - protect 2-3 deep work blocks minimum

### For Managers
- Cluster meetings on specific days (e.g., Wed/Fri)
- Reserve Mon/Tue/Thu mornings for strategic work
- Delegate ruthlessly to create space

### For Entrepreneurs
- Theme weeks: Week 1 = Product, Week 2 = Sales, Week 3 = Ops
- Longer deep work blocks (3-4 hours) fewer days per week
- Build business systems during shallow work time
```

## Example

**Input:**
I'm a software engineer working 9-5. I'm most focused in the morning. My main issues: too many meetings interrupt my coding time, and I spend 2+ hours/day on email and Slack. I want to ship my current feature by end of month.

**Output:**

[Full time blocking schedule as shown above, customized with:]

- 7-11 AM: Protected deep work for feature coding (4 hours)
- 11-12 PM: Shallow work (email, Slack batch processing)
- 1-2 PM: Meetings only (limit to 3 per week, decline rest)
- 2-4 PM: Secondary coding or code review
- 4-5 PM: Admin, planning, buffer

**Meeting policy:** "I batch meetings 1-2 PM. My mornings are blocked for coding. Can we find a time in my afternoon window?"

**Email policy:** Check email 3x daily (11 AM, 2 PM, 4:30 PM). Auto-responder: "I check email at 11 AM, 2 PM, and 4:30 PM. For urgent matters, Slack DM me."

**Slack policy:** Notifications snoozed 7-11 AM and 2-4 PM. Status: "🔴 Deep work - back at [time]"

## Tips for Use

- **Start small:** Protect just 1 deep work block per day for first week
- **Communicate boundaries:** Tell team about your new schedule in advance
- **Be consistent:** Time blocks work best when they're the same every day (habit formation)
- **Track violations:** Note when/why you broke time blocks - patterns reveal systemic issues
- **Adjust weekly:** Review and tweak during Friday review session
