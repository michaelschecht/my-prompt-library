# B2B SaaS LinkedIn Lead Generation Campaign

## Purpose
Create a structured LinkedIn campaign to generate high-quality leads for a B2B SaaS product, focusing on decision-makers in target industries.

## Instructions
Act as an expert B2B social media marketer. Develop a comprehensive 4-week LinkedIn campaign strategy for a B2B SaaS product. Include a mix of thought leadership posts, case study highlights, and direct response ads. Define the target audience criteria (job titles, industries, company size). Ensure the tone is professional, authoritative, and focused on ROI and solving specific business pain points.

Provide the details of the SaaS product (e.g., project management tool for enterprise, AI customer support platform) and the primary value proposition.

## Output Format
Provide the campaign strategy in the following structure:
1. **Campaign Overview & Goals**: Objective, target audience, and key metrics (KPIs).
2. **Weekly Content Schedule (Weeks 1-4)**: For each week, outline 3 organic posts and 1 ad concept, including:
   - Post Theme/Topic
   - Draft Copy
   - Suggested Media (e.g., infographic, video, carousel)
   - Relevant Hashtags
3. **Ad Targeting & Budget Allocation**: Recommendations for LinkedIn Ads targeting and budget split.
4. **Lead Magnet/Call to Action**: Strategy for capturing leads (e.g., whitepaper download, webinar registration).

## Example
**Input:**
SaaS Product: Cloud-based inventory management software.
Value Proposition: Reduces stockouts by 30% and automates reordering for mid-sized retailers.

**Output:**
### 1. Campaign Overview & Goals
**Objective:** Generate 150 qualified leads (demo requests) in 30 days.
**Target Audience:** Supply Chain Managers, Operations Directors, COOs in retail companies (50-500 employees).
**KPIs:** CTR on ads, Cost per Lead (CPL), Demo conversion rate.

### 2. Weekly Content Schedule (Week 1)
**Organic Post 1 (Thought Leadership):**
- **Theme:** The hidden cost of manual inventory tracking.
- **Copy:** "Still using spreadsheets for inventory? You might be losing up to 15% of your revenue to stockouts..."
- **Media:** Carousel comparing manual vs. automated tracking.

*(Continues for other posts and weeks)*
