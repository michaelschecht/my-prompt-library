# Freelance Marketplace Growth Campaign

## Purpose
Develop a dual-sided social media strategy to attract both high-quality freelancers and potential clients to a new or growing freelance marketplace.

## Instructions
Act as a social media strategist for a two-sided marketplace. Create a comprehensive campaign designed to balance the acquisition of "supply" (freelancers) and "demand" (clients). The strategy should primarily utilize Twitter and LinkedIn, focusing on building community, showcasing talent, and demonstrating value to businesses.

Specify the niche of the marketplace (e.g., vetted software developers, top-tier copywriters, creative designers) and the unique value proposition for both sides.

## Output Format
Provide the strategy in the following format:
1. **Marketplace Positioning**: The niche, value proposition for freelancers, and value proposition for clients.
2. **Dual-Sided Content Strategy**:
   - **For Freelancers (Supply)**: Content themes to attract and retain talent (e.g., tips for higher rates, community highlights).
   - **For Clients (Demand)**: Content themes to attract businesses (e.g., case studies, the cost of bad hires, speed of matching).
3. **Platform-Specific Tactics**:
   - **Twitter**: Threads, community building, and engaging with relevant discussions.
   - **LinkedIn**: Thought leadership, company updates, and B2B outreach.
4. **Key Activation Events**: Ideas for Twitter Spaces, LinkedIn Live sessions, or AMAs to drive engagement.

## Example
**Input:**
Niche: Vetted top 5% React developers.
Value for Freelancers: Guaranteed payment, high-quality projects.
Value for Clients: Pre-vetted talent, hire within 48 hours.

**Output:**
### 1. Marketplace Positioning
**Niche:** Premium React development talent.
**Freelancer USP:** "Focus on coding, we handle the clients and payments."
**Client USP:** "Scale your engineering team in 48 hours with guaranteed quality."

### 2. Dual-Sided Content Strategy
**For Freelancers:**
- Twitter threads on advanced React patterns.
- Highlighting successful projects completed by community members.

**For Clients:**
- LinkedIn posts detailing the cost of a bad technical hire.
- Case studies of startups that shipped features 2x faster using the marketplace.

*(Continues with specific tactics and activation events)*
