# AI Tool Beta Launch Twitter Campaign

## Purpose
Design a high-impact, viral Twitter campaign to generate excitement, waitlist signups, and early feedback for a new AI tool entering closed beta.

## Instructions
Act as a product marketer launching a cutting-edge AI tool. Develop a 14-day Twitter campaign specifically designed to drive waitlist signups and build a community of early adopters ("build in public" style). The campaign should leverage FOMO (fear of missing out), impressive use cases, and engaging visuals (videos/GIFs of the tool in action).

Detail the specific AI tool (e.g., AI video generator, automated coding assistant, AI presentation maker) and the primary problem it solves.

## Output Format
Outline the Twitter campaign structure as follows:
1. **Campaign Goals & Mechanics**: Target number of waitlist signups, mechanics for gaining early access (e.g., "RT for DM access").
2. **The "Build in Public" Narrative**: The story you will tell over the 14 days (from idea to beta).
3. **Daily Tweet Schedule (Days 1-14)**: Provide the specific type of tweet for each day, including:
   - **Day 1 (The Hook/Announcement)**: The viral initial tweet.
   - **Days 2-13 (Value & Proof)**: Showcasing features, addressing objections, sharing early user feedback.
   - **Day 14 (The Push)**: Final call to action before closing the initial batch.
4. **Engagement & Growth Tactics**: Strategies for replying to comments, engaging with larger accounts, and using Twitter threads effectively.

## Example
**Input:**
AI Tool: AI-powered automated slide deck generator from a simple text prompt.
Problem Solved: Hours wasted formatting presentations.

**Output:**
### 1. Campaign Goals & Mechanics
**Goal:** 5,000 waitlist signups.
**Mechanic:** "We're letting in 100 people today. Retweet this and reply 'deck' to get a DM with the access link."

### 2. The Narrative
"I spent 100 hours formatting slides last year. I built a tool so I never have to do it again. Here's how we're killing PowerPoint."

### 3. Daily Tweet Schedule
**Day 1 (The Hook):** "I typed 'pitch deck for a coffee shop startup' and got this in 3 seconds. [Video attached]. We're opening beta access to 100 people..."
**Day 2 (The Proof):** A thread detailing the prompt engineering behind the tool, showing a complex example.
**Day 3 (User Spotlight):** Retweeting an early beta user who created a deck in under a minute.

*(Continues with the rest of the schedule and tactics)*
