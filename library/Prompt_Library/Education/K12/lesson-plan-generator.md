# K-12 Lesson Plan Generator

Generate comprehensive, standards-aligned lesson plans for K-12 classrooms with differentiated instruction and assessment strategies.

## Prompt

```
You are an experienced K-12 educator specializing in standards-aligned lesson planning. Create a detailed lesson plan with the following structure:

**LESSON DETAILS:**
- Subject: [Subject]
- Grade Level: [Grade]
- Topic: [Topic]
- Duration: [Time]
- Standards: [State/Common Core Standards]

**LEARNING OBJECTIVES:**
Create 3-5 SMART objectives (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) that clearly state what students will know, understand, or be able to do by the end of the lesson.

**MATERIALS NEEDED:**
- List all required materials, technology, and resources
- Include links to digital resources where applicable
- Note any materials needed for differentiation

**LESSON STRUCTURE:**

1. **Warm-Up/Hook (5-10 minutes)**
   - Engaging activity to activate prior knowledge
   - Real-world connection or hook to capture interest

2. **Direct Instruction (15-20 minutes)**
   - Clear explanation of new content
   - Visual aids, demonstrations, or modeling
   - Check for understanding questions

3. **Guided Practice (15-20 minutes)**
   - Scaffolded activity with teacher support
   - Opportunities for students to practice new skills
   - Formative assessment checkpoints

4. **Independent Practice (15-20 minutes)**
   - Students apply learning independently
   - Choice board or tiered activities for differentiation

5. **Closure (5-10 minutes)**
   - Review key concepts
   - Exit ticket or quick assessment
   - Preview next lesson

**DIFFERENTIATION STRATEGIES:**
- **For Struggling Learners:** Modifications, scaffolds, sentence starters
- **For Advanced Learners:** Extension activities, enrichment challenges
- **For English Language Learners:** Visual supports, vocabulary pre-teaching
- **For Students with IEPs:** Specific accommodations

**ASSESSMENT:**
- **Formative:** Ongoing checks during lesson (thumbs up/down, whiteboard checks, etc.)
- **Summative:** Exit ticket or end-of-lesson assessment
- **Success Criteria:** How will you know students mastered the objectives?

**CROSS-CURRICULAR CONNECTIONS:**
- How does this lesson connect to other subjects?
- Real-world applications

**TECHNOLOGY INTEGRATION:**
- Tools or apps used (if applicable)
- Digital resources for extension or practice

**REFLECTION PROMPTS:**
- What worked well?
- What would you modify for next time?
- Which students need additional support?

Generate a complete lesson plan following this structure for: [SPECIFY YOUR LESSON]
```

## Example Use Cases

### Elementary Math (3rd Grade)
"Create a lesson plan on multiplication arrays for 3rd grade, aligned to Common Core standard 3.OA.A.3, duration 45 minutes."

### Middle School Science (7th Grade)
"Generate a lesson on the water cycle for 7th grade science, including hands-on lab activity and differentiation for special education students."

### High School English (11th Grade)
"Design a lesson analyzing symbolism in 'The Great Gatsby' for 11th grade AP English Literature, 90-minute block schedule."

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be Specific:** Include grade level, topic, duration, and any special needs
2. **Mention Standards:** Reference Common Core, Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS), or state standards
3. **Note Class Context:** Mention if you have ELL students, gifted students, or students with IEPs
4. **Request Formats:** Ask for "fillable template," "editable Google Doc format," or "printable PDF version"
5. **Add Constraints:** "No prep required," "uses only materials in classroom," "remote learning friendly"

## Advanced Variations

### Inquiry-Based Learning
"Create a lesson using the 5E model (Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate, Evaluate) for [topic]."

### Project-Based Learning
"Design a multi-day project-based learning unit on [topic] where students solve a real-world problem."

### Flipped Classroom
"Generate a flipped classroom lesson where students watch a video at home and engage in hands-on activities in class for [topic]."

### Culturally Responsive Teaching
"Create a lesson on [topic] that incorporates diverse perspectives and culturally relevant examples for a classroom with [demographics]."

## Bloom's Taxonomy Integration

Ensure your lesson includes activities at multiple cognitive levels:
- **Remember:** Recall facts (quizzes, flashcards)
- **Understand:** Explain concepts (summarize, paraphrase)
- **Apply:** Use knowledge (solve problems, demonstrate)
- **Analyze:** Break down information (compare/contrast, categorize)
- **Evaluate:** Make judgments (critique, defend, prioritize)
- **Create:** Produce something new (design, compose, invent)

## Output Formats

Request specific formats:
- **Editable:** "Provide in table format that I can copy to Google Docs"
- **Printable:** "Create a one-page printable lesson plan"
- **Digital:** "Generate with hyperlinks to digital resources"
- **Template:** "Give me a fillable template I can reuse"
