# LinkedIn Carousel Creator

## Purpose
LinkedIn carousels (PDF documents) are currently one of the highest-performing content formats on the platform, driving significant engagement and dwell time. This prompt transforms a raw idea, blog post, or topic into a perfectly structured script for a LinkedIn carousel, complete with slide-by-side visual instructions and hook-driven copywriting.

## Instructions

I want you to act as an expert B2B Content Marketer and LinkedIn Growth Strategist.

I will provide a [topic or source material]. Your task is to turn this into a highly engaging, 7-to-10 slide LinkedIn carousel script.

Follow these strict rules for the carousel structure:
1. **Slide 1 (The Hook):** Must stop the scroll. Needs a bold claim, a surprising statistic, or a counter-intuitive statement. Minimal text.
2. **Slide 2 (The Setup/Agitation):** Explain why the reader should care or agitate the problem they are facing.
3. **Slides 3-X (The Core Value):** 1 actionable point per slide. Keep text extremely brief (under 15 words per slide if possible). Use bullet points or short, punchy sentences.
4. **Second to Last Slide (The Summary):** A quick recap of the framework or main points.
5. **Final Slide (The CTA):** A clear Call to Action (e.g., "Follow for more", "Link in comments", "Save this post").

For each slide, provide:
- **Copy:** The exact text to put on the slide.
- **Visual:** A brief instruction for the designer on what imagery, icons, or layout to use.

Additionally, provide the accompanying text post (the "caption") that will go along with the carousel upload, including a hook and relevant hashtags.

**Topic or Source Material:** [Insert your topic, article URL, or raw thoughts here]
**Target Audience:** [e.g., SaaS Founders, Junior Developers, Sales Managers]
**Tone:** [e.g., Professional, Contrarian, Humorous, Educational]

## Output Format
The output should be formatted as a numbered list of slides, with bold headers for **Copy** and **Visual**, followed by a section titled **Accompanying LinkedIn Post**.

## Example

**Input:**
**Topic or Source Material:** Why most cold emails fail and 3 simple frameworks to fix them.
**Target Audience:** SDRs and B2B Sales Leaders
**Tone:** Punchy and Direct

**Output:**

Here is the script and caption for your LinkedIn Carousel:

### Carousel Script

**Slide 1**
**Copy:** 90% of your cold emails are deleted before they're opened. Here's why (and how to fix it).
**Visual:** Large, bold text on a dark background. An icon of a trash can with a notification badge.

**Slide 2**
**Copy:** The Problem: You're talking about YOU. "We do X", "Our product Y", "I'd love 15 mins". The prospect doesn't care.
**Visual:** Split screen. Left side: A generic email template crossed out in red. Right side: A confused-looking buyer persona.

**Slide 3**
**Copy:** The Fix: Flip the script. Talk about THEM. Here are 3 frameworks that actually book meetings. 👉
**Visual:** Arrow pointing right. Bright, contrasting background color to signal a shift in the presentation.

**Slide 4**
**Copy:** Framework 1: The "Observation + Insight"
**Visual:** Large text for the framework name. An icon of a magnifying glass.

**Slide 5**
**Copy:** Example: "Saw you're hiring 5 new AEs. Usually, that means ramp time is a bottleneck. We cut ramp time by 40%."
**Visual:** A mock email interface showing this exact text, with "Saw you're hiring" highlighted in yellow.

**Slide 6**
**Copy:** Framework 2: The "Pain + Peer Proof"
**Visual:** Large text for the framework name. An icon of two people shaking hands.

**Slide 7**
**Copy:** Example: "Most VP Sales struggle with forecast accuracy in Q4. We helped [Competitor Name] hit 98% accuracy."
**Visual:** A mock email interface. The competitor's name should be bolded.

**Slide 8**
**Copy:** Framework 3: The "Soft Ask"
**Visual:** Large text for the framework name. An icon of a question mark.

**Slide 9**
**Copy:** Stop asking for 15 minutes. Start asking: "Is this a priority for you right now?" or "Opposed to learning more?"
**Visual:** A big red X over "15 minutes" and a green checkmark next to the alternative questions.

**Slide 10**
**Copy:** Summary:
1. Make it about them.
2. Show you did research.
3. Lower the friction of the ask.
**Visual:** Clean, numbered list. Minimalist design.

**Slide 11**
**Copy:** Want more tactical sales advice? Hit Follow. 🔔 (And steal my top 5 email templates in the comments).
**Visual:** A large arrow pointing up to the top right corner (where the follow button is on LinkedIn profiles) and a smaller arrow pointing down to the comments.

### Accompanying LinkedIn Post

Your cold email is probably awful.

I review hundreds of pitches a week, and 90% of them make the exact same mistake:

They talk about themselves.

"We are the leading provider of..."
"Our revolutionary platform..."
"I would love 15 minutes of your time..."

Delete. Delete. Delete.

Your prospect doesn't care about your company. They care about their problems.

Swipe through the carousel to see the 3 frameworks we use to flip the script and actually book meetings.

(My favorite is the 'Soft Ask' on slide 8).

Which of these frameworks are you going to try today? Let me know below. 👇

## Tips for Use
- Once the text is generated, you can copy the "Copy" sections directly into tools like Canva or Figma, which have pre-built LinkedIn Carousel templates.
- Keep the number of slides between 5 and 12. Anything longer risks drop-off before the CTA.
