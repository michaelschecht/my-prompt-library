## Purpose

This prompt helps podcast producers and editors plan the musical journey of an episode. By analyzing the script, the AI identifies the emotional shifts, thematic sections, and pacing to recommend specific styles of background music and where tracks should change, fade, or swell.

---

## Input Requirements

**Required:**
- [PODCAST_SCRIPT]: The full text or segment of the podcast script.

**Optional:**
- [PODCAST_THEME]: The overarching theme or subject matter of the podcast.
- [MUSICAL_PREFERENCES]: Any specific instruments, genres, or artists that serve as inspiration.

---

## Prompt

```
You are an expert music supervisor and podcast producer with a keen ear for how music drives narrative and emotion.

I will provide you with a text-based podcast script. Your task is to design a comprehensive Background Music Score Plan for this episode.

Here is the podcast script:
[PODCAST_SCRIPT]

Podcast Theme: [PODCAST_THEME]
Musical Preferences/Inspiration: [MUSICAL_PREFERENCES]

Please read through the script and identify the emotional arc, segment changes, and pacing. Create a cue sheet that maps out the background music strategy.

For each distinct musical cue, provide:
1. The trigger point (the line of dialogue where the music should start, change, or stop).
2. The mood or emotion the music should convey (e.g., contemplative, energetic, mysterious).
3. Suggested genres, instrumentation, or tempo (e.g., Lo-fi hip hop, fast acoustic guitar, slow ambient synth).
4. Editing instructions (e.g., hard cut, slow crossfade, swell and dip under dialogue).

Ensure the suggestions flow logically and enhance the spoken word without overpowering it.
```

---

## Expected Output

A chronological cue sheet detailing the musical score for the podcast episode.

**Format:**
- Structured list or table

**Key components to include:**
- Cue Number
- Trigger / Location in script
- Desired Mood
- Instrumentation / Genre suggestion
- Transition / Editing Instructions

**Example structure:**
1. **Cue 1 (Intro):** [Details]
2. **Cue 2 (First Segment):** [Details]
3. **Cue 3 (Climax/Conclusion):** [Details]

## Example Usage

### Input
```
[PODCAST_SCRIPT]: "Host: We've talked about the history, the data, the failures. But what comes next? What is the future of this technology? Let's bring in our guest, Dr. Smith, to explain."
[PODCAST_THEME]: Technology and Innovation
[MUSICAL_PREFERENCES]: Electronic, Synthwave, Minimalist
```

### Output
```
### Music Score Plan

**Cue 1**
- **Trigger:** "...the history, the data, the failures."
- **Mood:** Reflective, slightly tense
- **Instrumentation:** Minimalist pulsing synth, low tempo
- **Instructions:** Play subtly underneath dialogue.

**Cue 2**
- **Trigger:** "But what comes next? What is the future..."
- **Mood:** Hopeful, forward-looking, energetic
- **Instrumentation:** Bright arpeggiated synths, upbeat electronic rhythm
- **Instructions:** Swell music on "What comes next?", hold for 2 seconds, then dip under the introduction of Dr. Smith.
```

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Tip 1:** When using stock music libraries, the "Mood" and "Instrumentation" descriptors generated by this prompt can be used directly as search keywords.
- **Tip 2:** Ensure there is enough variation in the music to keep the listener engaged, but avoid changing tracks so frequently that it becomes jarring.
- **Tip 3:** Pay special attention to "Editing instructions" to create professional-sounding transitions.

## Notes
