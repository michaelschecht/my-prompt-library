# Productivity Software Retention Campaign

## Purpose
Create an educational and community-focused social media campaign aimed at existing users of a productivity software to reduce churn, increase feature adoption, and build brand loyalty.

## Instructions
Act as a customer success marketing manager for a SaaS productivity tool (e.g., task manager, note-taking app, team collaboration software). Develop a 4-week retention campaign focused on helping existing users get more value out of the product. The strategy should shift focus from acquisition to education, highlighting advanced features, workflow optimizations, and power-user tips.

Detail the productivity tool's core function, the specific features that are underutilized but highly correlated with long-term retention, and the primary platforms used for customer engagement (e.g., Twitter, YouTube, a dedicated user forum/community).

## Output Format
Outline the retention campaign in the following structure:
1. **Retention Goals & Target Segments**: Objectives (e.g., increase weekly active usage by 15%), target segments (e.g., users who haven't logged in for 7 days, intermediate users ready for advanced features).
2. **"Power User" Content Series**: A structured series of educational content (e.g., "Workflow Wednesdays") focusing on:
   - Deep dives into complex features (e.g., automation, integrations, custom templates).
   - Video tutorials and quick-tip GIFs.
3. **Community Building & Support**:
   - **Twitter/LinkedIn AMAs**: Hosting Q&A sessions with the product team.
   - **User Spotlights**: Showcasing how real companies or individuals use the tool to save time.
4. **In-App & Email Integration**: How social media efforts tie into triggered emails and in-app product tours based on user behavior.

## Example
**Input:**
Tool: A robust note-taking and personal knowledge management (PKM) app.
Underutilized Features: Bi-directional linking and database views.
Engagement Platforms: Twitter, YouTube, and an active Discord community.

**Output:**
### 1. Retention Goals & Target Segments
**Goal:** Increase the adoption of database views by 25% among users active for more than 30 days.
**Target Segment:** "Intermediate" users who use basic notes but haven't explored relational data features.

### 2. "Power User" Content Series (Workflow Wednesdays)
**Week 1:** "Stop searching, start connecting." A short YouTube video demonstrating the power of bi-directional linking.
**Week 2:** Twitter thread: "5 ways to turn your messy notes into an organized database." Includes GIFs of creating Kanban and Calendar views.
