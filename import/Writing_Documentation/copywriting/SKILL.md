# Copywriting — Persuasion, Conversion & Brand Voice

Write copy that moves people to action — from landing page headlines to email sequences to product descriptions that sell.

## When to Use This Skill

**USE when:**
- Writing landing pages, sales pages, or product pages
- Crafting email campaigns (launch, nurture, re-engagement)
- Creating product descriptions for stores or marketplaces
- Writing CTAs, taglines, headlines, and ad copy
- Defining or applying brand voice and messaging

**DON'T USE when:**
- Writing documentation or guides → use `/technical-writing` or `/user-guides`
- Writing long-form reports → use `/report-writing`
- Defining editorial standards → use `/style-guide`

## Principle 1: The Copy Framework Stack

### Choose Your Framework

| Framework | Structure | Best For |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **AIDA** | Attention → Interest → Desire → Action | Landing pages, sales pages |
| **PAS** | Problem → Agitate → Solution | Email, blog intros, ads |
| **BAB** | Before → After → Bridge | Case studies, testimonials |
| **4 Ps** | Promise → Picture → Proof → Push | Long-form sales letters |
| **FAB** | Features → Advantages → Benefits | Product descriptions |

### AIDA Applied

```markdown
## ATTENTION (Headline — stop the scroll)
"Ship your SaaS in a weekend, not a quarter."

## INTEREST (Subhead + opening — hook them deeper)
"The average developer spends 6 weeks on auth, payments, and deployment
before writing a single line of product code."

## DESIRE (Body — make them want it)
"[Product] gives you auth, billing, deployment, and analytics out of
the box. Our customers launch 4x faster — and the last three
ProductHunt #1 Products were built on our stack."

## ACTION (CTA — tell them exactly what to do)
"Start building free → no credit card required"
```

### PAS Applied

```markdown
## PROBLEM (Name their pain — show you understand)
"You spend more time formatting reports than analyzing data."

## AGITATE (Twist the knife — make the pain vivid)
"Every Monday morning, it's the same: 2 hours copying numbers into
slides, fixing broken charts, chasing people for updates. By the
time the report is ready, the data is already stale."

## SOLUTION (Relief — your product is the answer)
"[Product] generates your weekly report automatically from live data.
One click. Always current. Reclaim your Mondays."
```

## Principle 2: Headlines

### Headline Formulas

```
THE "HOW TO" HEADLINE:
  "How to [desirable outcome] without [undesirable requirement]"
  → "How to grow your email list without writing a single blog post"

THE SPECIFIC NUMBER:
  "[Number] ways to [achieve goal] in [timeframe]"
  → "7 ways to cut your AWS bill in half this month"

THE QUESTION:
  "[Question that implies value]?"
  → "What if your CI pipeline took 30 seconds instead of 30 minutes?"

THE CONTRAST:
  "Stop [bad thing]. Start [good thing]."
  → "Stop guessing what users want. Start knowing."

THE TESTIMONIAL LEAD:
  "'[Quote from customer about specific result]'"
  → "'We shipped 3x faster after switching' — CTO, Acme Corp"

THE NEGATIVE:
  "Don't [common mistake] until you [read/try/see this]"
  → "Don't choose a database until you've read this comparison"
```

### Headline Rules

```
✓ Specific > vague ("Save 4 hours/week" > "Save time")
✓ Benefit > feature ("Write code faster" > "AI-powered autocomplete")
✓ Concrete > abstract ("10,000 developers" > "Trusted by many")
✓ Short > long (under 12 words for most headlines)
✓ Test 5+ headlines before choosing — your first idea is rarely best

POWER WORDS (use sparingly):
  Free, New, Proven, Instant, Easy, Secret, Guaranteed,
  Discover, Exclusive, Limited, Simple, Fast, Save, Now
```

## Principle 3: Body Copy

### Writing Persuasive Body Copy

```
RULE 1: LEAD WITH BENEFITS, NOT FEATURES
  Feature: "256-bit AES encryption"
  Benefit: "Your data is protected by bank-grade security"
  Best: "Sleep easy knowing your data has bank-grade encryption (AES-256)"

RULE 2: ONE IDEA PER PARAGRAPH
  Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences). White space is your friend.

  Online readers scan. They don't read walls of text.

  Like this.

RULE 3: USE "YOU" MORE THAN "WE"
  ✗ "We built an amazing platform that uses AI to..."
  ✓ "You get instant answers powered by AI that..."

RULE 4: SHOW, DON'T TELL
  ✗ "Our tool is incredibly fast"
  ✓ "Average query response: 47ms. That's faster than a blink."

RULE 5: ADDRESS OBJECTIONS
  "But what if it doesn't work for my use case?"
  → Handle this before the reader thinks it
```

### Social Proof Patterns

```
CUSTOMER QUOTE:
  "We reduced deployment time from 4 hours to 12 minutes."
  — Sarah Chen, VP Engineering at Acme Corp

METRIC PROOF:
  "50,000+ developers. 99.99% uptime. 4.8★ on G2."

LOGO BAR:
  [Company logos of recognizable customers]

CASE STUDY SNIPPET:
  "Acme Corp cut their infrastructure costs by 62% in the first
  quarter after migrating to [Product]. Read the full story →"

AUTHORITY:
  "Featured in TechCrunch, Wired, and The Verge"
  "Winner of the 2025 DevTools Award"
```

## Principle 4: Calls to Action (CTAs)

### CTA Hierarchy

```
PRIMARY CTA (one per page — the main conversion):
  Style: Large button, high contrast, above the fold AND at the bottom
  Copy: Specific action + value
  ✓ "Start building free"
  ✓ "Get your report in 60 seconds"
  ✓ "Try it free — no credit card"
  ✗ "Submit"
  ✗ "Click here"
  ✗ "Learn more" (for primary CTA)

SECONDARY CTA (lower commitment alternative):
  Style: Text link or ghost button
  Copy: "See a demo", "View pricing", "Read the docs"
  → For visitors not ready to commit to the primary action

MICRO CTA (within content):
  Inline text links: "See how Acme Corp saved $2M →"
  → Keeps engagement without interrupting flow
```

### CTA Button Copy Formula

```
[Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Urgency/Qualifier]

GOOD:
  "Start your free trial"
  "Download the 2026 report"
  "Book a 15-minute demo"
  "Get instant access"
  "Create your first project"

GREAT (adds specificity or value):
  "Start building in 2 minutes — free"
  "See your savings estimate"
  "Join 10,000+ developers"
  "Generate your report now"
```

## Principle 5: Email Copy

### Email Campaign Templates

```
LAUNCH EMAIL:
  Subject: [Product news] + [specific benefit/number]
  Body structure:
    Line 1: The news (what's new)
    Line 2-3: Why it matters to THEM
    Line 4-5: Key features (bullet points)
    CTA: Try it / Learn more
    P.S.: Urgency or bonus

NURTURE EMAIL:
  Subject: [Question] or [Helpful tip]
  Body structure:
    Line 1: Acknowledge their situation
    Line 2-4: Teach something valuable (no selling)
    Line 5: Soft bridge to product
    CTA: Optional, low-pressure

RE-ENGAGEMENT EMAIL:
  Subject: "We miss you" or "Things have changed"
  Body structure:
    Line 1: Acknowledge absence without guilt
    Line 2-3: What's new since they left (specific features)
    CTA: "Come back and see what's new"
    Unsubscribe: Prominent and respectful
```

### Email Subject Line Rules

```
✓ Under 50 characters (mobile preview)
✓ Specific > clever ("Your report is ready" > "You won't believe this")
✓ Personalization if genuine ("[Name], your usage report")
✓ Create curiosity gap WITHOUT clickbait
✓ Test 2-3 subject lines per send (A/B test)

✗ ALL CAPS
✗ Excessive punctuation!!!
✗ Misleading content (kills trust permanently)
✗ "Newsletter" or "Update" (boring, gets archived)
✗ Spam trigger words overuse (free, act now, limited time)
```

### E-commerce Product Copy Template

```markdown

**[One-line value proposition]**

- **[Feature]** — [Benefit to the buyer]

### Specifications

|------|--------|
| [Dimension] | [Value] |
| [Material] | [Value] |

### What's Included

- [Item 2]
- [Item 3]
```

### SaaS Product Copy

```markdown
## [Feature/Plan Name]

**[Headline benefit — what they can DO, not what it IS]**

[1-2 sentences expanding on the benefit with specifics]

✓ [Included capability 1]
✓ [Included capability 2]

```

## Output

Save copy to the appropriate deliverable format:
- Landing pages: `copy/landing-[page-name].md`
- Emails: `copy/email-[campaign]-[sequence-number].md`
- Product descriptions: `copy/product-[name].md`

## Guidelines

- **Benefits over features, always** — the customer doesn't care how it works, they care what it does for them
- **Write like you talk** — then edit for clarity. Robotic copy doesn't convert.
- Specificity is persuasion — "4.8 stars from 2,300 reviews" beats "highly rated"
- Every piece of copy needs ONE primary CTA — don't give readers 5 choices
- Test everything — headlines, CTAs, subject lines. Your intuition is wrong 40% of the time.
- Use `/humanizer` to strip AI-generated patterns from drafted copy
- Use `/style-guide` to maintain brand voice consistency
- Use `/technical-writing` when copy needs to also educate (product docs that sell)
