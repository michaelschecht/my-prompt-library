# 📈 Financial ROI Calculator

## Prerequisites
Input data regarding current costs, proposed solution costs, and expected efficiency gains or revenue increases.

## Usage
Use this skill during the late stages of a sales cycle to build a compelling business case for a prospect.

## Configuration
Provide specific metrics (e.g., hourly wage of employees, time saved per task).

## Examples
**Input:** "Calculate ROI. Current state: 5 employees spend 10 hours a week on manual data entry. Wage is $30/hr. Our software costs $1000/month and reduces data entry time by 80%."

**Output:**
### ROI Calculation
**Current Cost:** 5 employees * 10 hrs * 4 weeks * $30/hr = $6,000 / month.
**New Time Spent:** 2 hours/week per employee.
**New Labor Cost:** 5 * 2 hrs * 4 weeks * $30/hr = $1,200 / month.
**Total Monthly Cost with Software:** $1,200 (labor) + $1,000 (software) = $2,200 / month.
**Monthly Savings:** $6,000 - $2,200 = $3,800.
**Annual Savings:** $45,600.
**ROI:** (($3,800 - $1,000) / $1,000) * 100 = 280% monthly ROI.

## Advanced Usage
Generate a multi-year NPV (Net Present Value) calculation factoring in inflation and software price increases.

## Integration
Integrates with presentation skills to auto-generate ROI slides.

## Troubleshooting
Ensure all inputs are standardized to the same time period (e.g., all monthly or all annual) to avoid math hallucinations by the LLM.

## Best Practices
Always explicitly state the assumptions used in the calculation so the prospect can validate them.

## Performance Considerations
High accuracy required; use models with strong reasoning capabilities.
