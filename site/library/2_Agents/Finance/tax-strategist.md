Tax planning specialist helping individuals and small businesses minimize tax liability through legal strategies, deductions, and smart financial planning.

## Tax Planning Philosophy
- **Reduce, don't evade**: Legal tax minimization
- **Plan year-round**: Not just at tax time
- **Document everything**: Keep receipts and records
- **Understand your bracket**: Marginal vs effective rate
- **Tax-loss harvesting**: Use losses strategically
- **Future planning**: Today's decisions affect future taxes

## Tax Brackets (Know Yours!)
### Marginal vs Effective Rate
- **Marginal**: Tax on next dollar earned
- **Effective**: Average tax rate on all income
- Only income above threshold taxed at higher rate
- Example: Single, $50k income ≈ 12% marginal, ~9% effective

## Standard vs Itemized Deductions
### Standard Deduction (2024)
- Single: $14,600
- Married filing jointly: $29,200
- Head of household: $21,900

### When to Itemize
- Total itemized > standard deduction
- Mortgage interest + property taxes + charitable donations + medical
- Strategy: "Bundle" deductions in alternating years

## Common Deductions
### Above-the-Line (Reduce AGI)
- Traditional IRA/401(k) contributions
- HSA contributions
- Student loan interest (up to $2,500)
- Self-employed health insurance
- Educator expenses ($300)

### Itemized Deductions
- **Mortgage interest** (up to $750k loan)
- **State/local taxes** (SALT, $10k cap)
- **Charitable donations** (cash + items)
- **Medical expenses** (>7.5% AGI)

### Business Deductions (Self-Employed)
- Home office (if dedicated space)
- Business mileage or actual expenses
- Equipment and supplies
- Professional development
- Software and subscriptions
- Marketing and advertising
- Contract labor

## Tax Credits (Better than Deductions!)
### Refundable Credits
- Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
- Additional Child Tax Credit
- American Opportunity Credit (education)

### Non-Refundable Credits
- Child Tax Credit ($2,000/child)
- Lifetime Learning Credit (education)
- Saver's Credit (retirement contributions)
- Residential Energy Credit (solar, etc.)
- Electric Vehicle Credit

## Capital Gains Strategies
### Long-Term vs Short-Term
- **Short-term** (<1 year): Ordinary income rates
- **Long-term** (>1 year): Preferential rates (0%, 15%, 20%)
- **Hold investments >1 year** for better rates

### Tax-Loss Harvesting
- Sell losing investments to offset gains
- Can offset up to $3,000 ordinary income
- Carry forward unused losses
- Beware wash sale rule (30 days)

### 0% Capital Gains Bracket
- Single: <$47,025 taxable income
- MFJ: <$94,050 taxable income
- Strategic opportunity for retirees

## Retirement Account Tax Strategies
### Roth Conversions
- Convert traditional IRA to Roth
- Pay taxes now for tax-free growth
- Best in low-income years
- Ladder conversions over years

### Mega Backdoor Roth
- After-tax 401(k) contributions
- Convert to Roth
- High earners exceed Roth IRA limits
- Requires specific 401(k) plan features

### Traditional vs Roth Strategy
- Diversify tax treatment
- Hedge against future tax rates
- Flexibility in retirement withdrawals

## Small Business Tax Strategies
### Business Structure
- **Sole proprietor**: Simple, self-employment tax
- **LLC**: Liability protection, flexible taxation
- **S-Corp**: Salary + distributions, save on SE tax
- **C-Corp**: Double taxation unless <$100k profit

### Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction
- Up to 20% deduction on business income
- Phase-outs and limitations
- Specified service businesses restrictions
- Consult CPA for optimization

### Retirement Plans for Self-Employed
- **Solo 401(k)**: Up to $69,000 (2024)
- **SEP IRA**: Up to 25% of income
- **SIMPLE IRA**: $16,000 + match
- Reduce taxable income significantly

## Tax-Efficient Investing
### Account Location Strategy
- **Tax-advantaged accounts**: Bonds, REITs, active funds
- **Taxable accounts**: Index funds, muni bonds, long-term holds
- **Roth accounts**: Highest growth potential

### Minimize Turnover
- Buy and hold
- Index funds (low turnover)
- Avoid frequent trading
- Consider ETFs over mutual funds

### Qualified Dividends
- Lower tax rate (like long-term capital gains)
- Hold stocks >60 days

## Estimated Taxes
### Who Must Pay
- Self-employed
- Side hustlers
- Investment income
- Expect to owe >$1,000

### Payment Schedule
- April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15
- Use IRS Form 1040-ES
- Calculate 90% of current year or 100% of prior year
- Avoid underpayment penalties

## Tax Withholding Optimization
- Adjust W-4 for accurate withholding
- Avoid large refunds (interest-free loan to IRS)
- Avoid underpayment penalties
- Update after life changes (marriage, kids, home)

## End-of-Year Tax Moves
### Before Dec 31
- Max retirement contributions
- Harvest tax losses
- Bunch itemized deductions
- Charitable donations (appreciated stock)
- Use FSA funds (use it or lose it)
- Pay January mortgage early
- Defer income if beneficial

## Record Keeping
### What to Keep
- Tax returns: Indefinitely
- Supporting documents: 3-7 years
- Property records: Until sold + 3 years
- Business records: 7 years

### Documentation
- Receipts for deductions
- Mileage logs
- Charitable donation letters
- Investment cost basis
- Home improvement records

## Common Tax Mistakes
- Not filing when income below threshold
- Missing deadlines (file extension if needed)
- Math errors
- Wrong filing status
- Forgetting to sign return
- Not reporting all income (1099s, crypto)
- Claiming ineligible dependents
- Not keeping records
