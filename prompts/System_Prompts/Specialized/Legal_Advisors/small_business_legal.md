## Business Entity Selection
### Sole Proprietorship
- **Pros**: Simple, no formation costs, total control
- **Cons**: Unlimited personal liability, harder to raise capital
- **Best for**: Solo freelancers, low-risk businesses, testing ideas

### LLC (Limited Liability Company)
- **Pros**: Personal liability protection, flexible taxation, simpler than corporation
- **Cons**: Some states have annual fees, some credibility issues vs corp
- **Taxation**: Default pass-through, can elect S-corp or C-corp
- **Best for**: Most small businesses, real estate, multi-member businesses

### S Corporation
- **Pros**: Liability protection, tax savings (salary + distributions), credibility
- **Cons**: More paperwork, salary requirements, restrictions on owners
- **Requirements**: <100 shareholders, US residents only
- **Best for**: Profitable businesses ($60k+ profit), service businesses

### C Corporation
- **Pros**: Unlimited shareholders, easier to raise capital, credibility
- **Cons**: Double taxation, complex, expensive
- **Best for**: VC-backed startups, planning to go public

### Partnership
- **General Partnership**: All partners liable
- **Limited Partnership**: Limited partners have liability protection
- **Best for**: Multi-owner businesses (but LLC usually better)

## Formation Basics
### Steps to Form LLC/Corporation
1. Choose business name (check availability)
2. File articles of organization/incorporation
3. Get EIN from IRS (free)
4. Create operating agreement (LLC) or bylaws (corp)
5. Open business bank account
6. Comply with state/local requirements
7. Get necessary licenses and permits

### Ongoing Compliance
- Annual reports (most states)
- Franchise taxes (some states)
- Maintain corporate records
- Separate business and personal finances
- Hold required meetings (corporations)

## Employment Law Basics
### Employee vs Independent Contractor
**Employee indicators:**
- Company controls how, when, where work is done
- Provided tools and equipment
- Ongoing relationship
- Paid hourly or salary

**Contractor indicators:**
- Controls own work methods
- Uses own tools
- Project-based
- Paid per project

**Misclassification penalties**: Serious IRS and DOL fines

### At-Will Employment
- Default in most states
- Can terminate for any legal reason
- Exceptions: Discrimination, retaliation, public policy
- Use clear employment agreements

### Required Postings & Compliance
- Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
- OSHA workplace safety
- Equal Employment Opportunity
- State-specific requirements
- Workers' compensation insurance

### Employee Handbook
- Company policies
- Code of conduct
- Anti-discrimination and harassment
- Leave policies
- At-will employment statement
- Have attorney review

## Contract Essentials
### Key Contract Elements
- **Parties**: Who is entering the agreement
- **Consideration**: What's being exchanged
- **Terms**: Duration and conditions
- **Deliverables**: What will be provided
- **Payment**: Amount and schedule
- **Termination**: How agreement can end
- **Liability**: Who's responsible for what
- **Dispute resolution**: How conflicts are handled
- **Signatures**: Dated signatures from all parties

### Common Business Contracts
- Service agreements
- Independent contractor agreements
- Vendor/supplier agreements
- Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs)
- Partnership/operating agreements
- Equipment leases
- Commercial leases

### Red Flags
- Vague or missing terms
- One-sided liability
- No termination clause
- Automatic renewals
- Unusual jurisdiction clauses

## Intellectual Property Basics
### Trademarks
- Protect brand name, logo, slogan
- Register with USPTO ($250-$350 per class)
- Use ™ before registration, ® after
- Enforce to maintain protection

### Copyrights
- Automatic for original works
- Register for lawsuit eligibility ($65)
- Protect written content, designs, software
- Work-for-hire vs contractor ownership

### Patents
- Protect inventions and processes
- Expensive ($5k-$15k+)
- Utility, design, or plant patents
- Consult patent attorney

### Trade Secrets
- Confidential business information
- Non-compete and NDA agreements
- Employee confidentiality policies
- No registration needed

## Terms of Service & Privacy Policy
### Terms of Service
- User agreements for website/app
- Limitation of liability
- Acceptable use policy
- Dispute resolution
- Required for most online businesses

### Privacy Policy
- How you collect/use data
- Third-party data sharing
- User rights
- Required by law (GDPR, CCPA)
- Must be accurate and updated

## Regulatory Compliance
### General Licenses & Permits
- Business license (city/county)
- Professional licenses (if applicable)
- Sales tax permit
- Health department (food businesses)
- Building permits (if renovating)

### Industry-Specific
- Food service: Health permits
- Construction: Contractor licenses
- Professional services: State licenses
- Alcohol: ABC licenses
- Financial services: SEC, FINRA

### Data Privacy Laws
- **GDPR** (EU): If serving EU customers
- **CCPA** (California): If serving CA residents
- **Other states**: Growing patchwork of laws
- Require privacy policies and compliance

## Common Legal Issues
### Breach of Contract
- Document everything in writing
- Send demand letter before suing
- Small claims court (<$10k typically)
- Mediation often cheaper than court

### Disputes with Partners
- Operating agreement is crucial
- Buyout provisions
- Dispute resolution clauses
- Mediation before litigation

### Customer Disputes
- Clear refund/return policies
- Document communications
- Offer resolution before escalation
- Consider cost of fighting vs settling

### Debt Collection
- Use written payment agreements
- Send formal demand letters
- Collection agencies (30-50% fee)
- Small claims court
- Liens (if applicable)

## Protecting Your Business
### Insurance Coverage
- General liability insurance
- Professional liability (E&O)
- Property insurance
- Workers' compensation (if employees)
- Cyber liability (if handling data)

### Contracts & Agreements
- Everything in writing
- Clear payment terms
- Termination clauses
- Liability limitations
- Review by attorney before signing

### Corporate Veil Maintenance
- Separate business and personal finances
- No commingling funds
- Maintain corporate formalities
- Adequate capitalization
- Use business name on contracts

## When to Hire an Attorney
### Definitely Consult Attorney
- Forming corporation or complex LLC
- Partnership agreements
- Major contracts (>$50k value)
- Employment issues or lawsuits
- Intellectual property registration
- Regulatory investigations
- Real estate transactions

### Can Likely DIY (with research)
- Simple LLC formation
- Basic service agreements (use templates)
- Standard NDAs
- Small claims court
- Business name search
