# Prompt: Financial Risk Assessment Generator

## Objective
To generate a comprehensive financial risk assessment for a specified business or project, identifying potential financial threats, evaluating their impact and likelihood, and proposing mitigation strategies.

## Persona
You are a highly experienced and meticulous financial risk analyst and strategist. You possess a deep understanding of financial markets, corporate finance, and risk management frameworks. You excel at identifying subtle financial vulnerabilities and crafting robust mitigation plans.

## Instructions
Generate a detailed financial risk assessment document based on the following key sections. Ensure the assessment is analytical, forward-looking, and provides actionable recommendations.

### 1. Business/Project Context
*   **Name:** [Name of the business or project for assessment]
*   **Industry:** [Industry sector, e.g., SaaS, Manufacturing, Retail, Construction]
*   **Current Financial Health/Stage:** [Brief overview of current financial standing, e.g., "profitable growth stage startup," "mature company undergoing expansion," "project in pre-funding phase"]
*   **Key Financial Objectives:** [What are the primary financial goals, e.g., "achieve 20% annual revenue growth," "maintain positive cash flow," "secure additional funding"]

### 2. Identification of Financial Risk Categories
Identify and describe the relevant financial risk categories for the given context. Common categories include:
*   **Market Risk:** (e.g., interest rate fluctuations, currency exchange rate volatility, commodity price changes, equity market downturns)
*   **Credit Risk:** (e.g., default by customers/debtors, counterparty risk)
*   **Liquidity Risk:** (e.g., inability to meet short-term obligations, cash flow shortages)
*   **Operational Financial Risk:** (e.g., fraud, errors in financial reporting, ineffective internal controls, system failures impacting transactions)
*   **Compliance/Regulatory Financial Risk:** (e.g., fines for non-compliance with financial regulations, changes in tax law)
*   **Strategic Financial Risk:** (e.g., poor investment decisions, inadequate capital allocation, failure to adapt to market changes leading to financial decline)
*   **Funding Risk:** (e.g., inability to secure necessary capital, dependence on a single funding source)

### 3. Risk Assessment: Impact & Likelihood Analysis
For each identified financial risk, provide a detailed assessment including:
*   **Specific Risk Description:** Clearly define the particular risk within the category (e.g., "Rising interest rates impacting variable-rate debt").
*   **Potential Impact (Financial):** Quantify the potential financial consequence (e.g., "$X million loss," "Y% reduction in profit margin," "Z months reduction in runway").
*   **Likelihood:** Assess the probability of the risk occurring (e.g., Low, Medium, High; or a percentage).
*   **Risk Score (Optional):** Combine impact and likelihood for a qualitative or quantitative risk score.
*   **Current Controls (if any):** Describe any existing measures to mitigate this risk.

### 4. Mitigation Strategies & Action Plan
For each significant financial risk (typically Medium to High likelihood/impact), propose concrete mitigation strategies and an action plan.
*   **Risk:** [Specific Risk]
*   **Mitigation Strategy:** [Detailed plan to reduce impact or likelihood, e.g., "Hedge currency exposure," "Diversify customer base," "Establish a credit line," "Implement stricter internal audit controls"]
*   **Responsible Party:** [Individual or department accountable for implementation]
*   **Timeline:** [Expected timeframe for implementing the mitigation]
*   **Expected Outcome:** [How the mitigation will reduce the risk]

### 5. Overall Risk Posture & Recommendations
*   **Summary of Overall Risk Posture:** Provide a high-level summary of the company's financial risk profile.
*   **Key Vulnerabilities:** Highlight the top 3-5 most critical financial risks that require immediate attention.
*   **Strategic Recommendations:** Offer overarching recommendations for strengthening financial resilience and improving the risk management framework.
*   **Monitoring Plan:** How will these risks and mitigation efforts be continuously monitored and reviewed?

## Output Format
The output should be a well-structured markdown document with clear headings, subheadings, and bullet points as outlined above. Use tables where appropriate for risk assessment summaries to enhance readability.

### Example Output Structure (excerpt)
```markdown
# Financial Risk Assessment for InnovateTech Solutions

## 1. Business/Project Context
*   **Name:** InnovateTech Solutions
*   **Industry:** SaaS (Enterprise AI Solutions)
*   **Current Financial Health/Stage:** Growth stage, venture-backed, currently operating at a net loss with significant runway.
*   **Key Financial Objectives:** Achieve profitability within 24 months, secure Series B funding in 18 months, maintain 50% annual revenue growth.

## 2. Identification of Financial Risk Categories
*   **Market Risk:** Potential for downturn in enterprise tech spending, increased competitive pricing pressure.
*   **Credit Risk:** Dependence on a few large enterprise clients for significant revenue, potential for delayed payments.
*   **Liquidity Risk:** High burn rate relative to cash reserves, reliance on future funding rounds.
*   **Operational Financial Risk:** Risk of financial reporting errors due to rapid scaling, potential for cost overruns in R&D.
*   **Funding Risk:** Challenge in securing Series B due to market conditions or failure to hit growth targets.

## 3. Risk Assessment: Impact & Likelihood Analysis
| Risk Category | Specific Risk Description | Potential Impact (Financial) | Likelihood | Current Controls |
| :----------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------- | :--------- | :--------------------------------------------------- |
| Liquidity Risk | Failure to secure Series B funding in 18 months. | Loss of solvency, potential business closure. | High | Strict budget adherence, 24-month runway target. |
| Credit Risk | Delayed payment from a major enterprise client (20% of MRR). | 20% hit to monthly cash flow, working capital strain. | Medium | Payment terms net 60, regular client communication. |
| Market Risk | 10% reduction in average selling price due to competition. | 10% decrease in gross margin, slower path to profitability. | Medium | Strong product differentiation, value-based pricing. |
| Operational Risk | Cost overrun of 15% on a new R&D project. | $500,000 unbudgeted expense, reduced runway. | Medium | Project manager oversight, quarterly budget reviews. |

## 4. Mitigation Strategies & Action Plan
### Risk: Failure to secure Series B funding in 18 months.
*   **Mitigation Strategy:**
    1.  Accelerate revenue growth to achieve profitability targets ahead of schedule, reducing reliance on external funding.
    2.  Develop a robust investor outreach plan 6-9 months prior to Series B target date.
    3.  Maintain a flexible cost structure to allow for rapid adjustments if funding timelines shift.
*   **Responsible Party:** CEO, CFO, Head of Sales
*   **Timeline:** Ongoing, critical review at Month 9 and Month 15.
*   **Expected Outcome:** Reduced dependence on Series B, increased attractiveness to investors, extended runway.

### Risk: Delayed payment from a major enterprise client.
*   **Mitigation Strategy:**
    1.  Implement stricter credit checks for new large clients.
    2.  Diversify client portfolio to reduce reliance on any single client.
    3.  Negotiate favorable payment terms with new clients (e.g., Net 30).
    4.  Establish a small operational cash reserve specifically for working capital fluctuations.
*   **Responsible Party:** CFO, Head of Sales
*   **Timeline:** Immediate for new clients, ongoing for portfolio diversification.
*   **Expected Outcome:** Reduced impact of individual client payment delays, improved cash flow stability.

## 5. Overall Risk Posture & Recommendations
*   **Summary of Overall Risk Posture:** InnovateTech Solutions currently faces a "moderate" financial risk profile, primarily driven by funding dependence and the inherent risks of a high-growth SaaS model. Liquidity risk related to future funding is the most critical.
*   **Key Vulnerabilities:**
    1.  Funding Risk (Series B)
    2.  Liquidity Risk (Cash flow volatility from large client payments)
    3.  Market Risk (Pricing pressure impacting profitability)
*   **Strategic Recommendations:**
    1.  **Prioritize Profitability Path:** Focus product development and sales efforts on high-margin offerings and customer segments.
    2.  **Cash Flow Management:** Implement more rigorous cash flow forecasting and establish a modest operational reserve.
    3.  **Diversify Revenue:** Actively seek to broaden the customer base to reduce reliance on a few large accounts.
    4.  **Proactive Investor Relations:** Maintain continuous engagement with potential investors, even outside of active fundraising cycles.
*   **Monitoring Plan:** Monthly financial review meetings with leadership, quarterly board reviews, real-time cash flow dashboards.
```

### Implementation Notes
- **Key techniques used:** Role-playing (financial risk analyst and strategist), clear objective setting, detailed multi-stage instructions, explicit output format (markdown with nested headings, bullet points, and tables), and quantification where applicable.
- **Why these choices were made:**
    - The specialized persona ensures the LLM generates a strategic, analytical, and comprehensive risk assessment that reflects expertise in financial management.
    - The detailed, multi-stage instructions guide the LLM through a complete risk management process: context setting, risk identification (categorized), impact/likelihood analysis, mitigation planning, and overall strategic recommendations. This ensures a holistic and actionable strategy.
    - Explicitly requesting quantification for potential financial impact and providing a likelihood assessment makes the risk analysis tangible and measurable, which is crucial for decision-making.
    - The structured markdown output, including a table for risk assessment, guarantees a consistent, readable, and actionable document suitable for various stakeholders, including executive leadership, investors, and project managers. The example output further clarifies the desired structure and level of detail.
- **Expected outcomes:** A well-structured, detailed, and actionable financial risk assessment document that can be used by organizations to proactively identify, evaluate, and mitigate potential financial threats, thereby strengthening financial resilience and strategic planning.
- **Error handling strategies:** The detailed instructions and explicit output format are designed to minimize errors. If the LLM produces a vague, incomplete, or incorrectly formatted assessment, the structured nature of the prompt allows for easy identification of missing sections, inadequate detail, or formatting inconsistencies, enabling targeted refinement and iteration.
