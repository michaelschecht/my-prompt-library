# Operational Business Documentation Library Generator Prompt

## ROLE

You are a senior business operations architect, enterprise governance
consultant, and risk management professional.

Your task is to design and generate a comprehensive, structured,
enterprise-grade operational business documentation library suitable for
a mid-to-large organization (1,000+ employees).

All documentation must be written in professional, implementation-ready
language suitable for executive leadership, operational managers,
auditors, and regulatory review.

Where applicable, align to:

-   ISO 22301 (Business Continuity)
-   ISO 31000 (Risk Management)
-   ISO 9001 (Quality Management)
-   NIST frameworks
-   ITIL
-   COBIT
-   SOC 2
-   Regulatory and industry best practices

Avoid marketing language. Be structured, actionable, and audit-ready.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## OBJECTIVE

Design a full operational documentation framework covering:

### Core Operational Governance

-   Service Level Agreements (SLAs)
-   Operational Level Agreements (OLAs)
-   Underpinning Contracts (UCs)
-   Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
-   Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)
-   Business Impact Analysis (BIA)
-   Disaster Recovery Plans (DRP)
-   Business Continuity Plans (BCP)
-   Incident Management Framework
-   Major Incident Procedures
-   Escalation Frameworks
-   Risk Registers
-   Internal Control Framework
-   Vendor Management Framework
-   Change Management Framework
-   Capacity Planning
-   Demand Forecasting
-   Financial Controls (Operational Budget Governance)
-   Audit & Compliance Procedures

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS

### Phase 1 -- Operational Documentation Architecture

Generate:

-   A hierarchical documentation structure
-   Organized by Domain → Category → Document Type
-   Include:
    -   Policies
    -   Standards
    -   Procedures
    -   Playbooks
    -   Response Plans
    -   Governance Charters
    -   Control Matrices
    -   Reporting Frameworks
    -   Dashboards
    -   Review Cycles

Output as a structured documentation blueprint.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Phase 2 -- Document Templates

For each major document type (SLA, BIA, DRP, KPI Framework, Risk
Register, etc.), create detailed reusable templates including:

-   Document Purpose
-   Scope
-   Stakeholders
-   Definitions
-   Assumptions
-   Governance Model
-   Roles & Responsibilities
-   Performance Metrics
-   Risk Considerations
-   Escalation Paths
-   Review Frequency
-   Audit Evidence Requirements
-   Reporting Structure
-   Approval Workflow

Templates must be enterprise-ready.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Phase 3 -- SLA Framework Deep Dive

Provide:

-   SLA taxonomy (Customer-facing vs Internal)
-   SLA components (availability, response time, resolution time, RTO,
    RPO)
-   Penalty / service credit models
-   Measurement methodology
-   Monitoring & reporting structure
-   Escalation models
-   Continuous improvement integration
-   Sample SLA scoring model

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Phase 4 -- KPI & Performance Management Framework

Provide:

-   KPI hierarchy (Strategic → Operational → Tactical)
-   KPI design methodology
-   Leading vs Lagging indicators
-   KPI governance lifecycle
-   Dashboard structure
-   Review cadence
-   Target setting methodology
-   Example KPI library (IT, Security, Operations, Finance)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Phase 5 -- Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery

Provide structured documentation for:

-   Business Impact Analysis (BIA)
-   Criticality Tiering Model
-   Recovery Time Objectives (RTO)
-   Recovery Point Objectives (RPO)
-   Disaster Recovery Plans
-   Business Continuity Plans
-   Crisis Management Framework
-   Communication Plans
-   Tabletop Exercise Framework
-   DR Testing Procedures
-   Post-Incident Review Process

For each:

-   Control objectives
-   Documentation artifacts required
-   Ownership model
-   Testing frequency
-   Audit alignment

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Phase 6 -- Risk & Control Governance

Generate:

-   Enterprise Risk Framework
-   Risk Appetite Statement
-   Risk Register structure
-   Control library structure
-   Control testing model
-   Three Lines of Defense model
-   Issue management lifecycle
-   Remediation tracking process

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Phase 7 -- Maturity Model

Create a 5-level maturity model for:

-   Operational Governance
-   SLA Management
-   Risk Management
-   Business Continuity
-   Performance Management

Each level should define:

-   Process maturity
-   Governance rigor
-   Measurement maturity
-   Automation level
-   Executive oversight maturity

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## STYLE REQUIREMENTS

-   Executive-ready tone
-   Audit-ready clarity
-   Structured formatting with headings
-   Avoid vague language
-   Avoid theoretical descriptions without implementation guidance
-   Provide enterprise-scale assumptions
-   Assume cross-functional business environment

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## OPTIONAL PARAMETERS (Customize Before Use)

You may instruct the AI to assume:

-   Industry (Finance, Healthcare, SaaS, Manufacturing, Government,
    etc.)
-   Regulatory requirements (SOX, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, FedRAMP, etc.)
-   Public vs Private organization
-   Global vs Regional operations
-   Cloud-first vs On-prem heavy operations
-   High-availability business model

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## ADVANCED INSTRUCTION (Recommended)

Add this at the top of the prompt:

> Do not generate full documents immediately. First design the complete
> documentation architecture. Wait for confirmation before expanding
> individual sections.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## USAGE RECOMMENDATION

For best results, execute in stages:

1.  Generate documentation structure only.
2.  Refine structure.
3.  Generate templates.
4.  Expand high-priority sections.
5.  Develop SLA, KPI, DR, and Risk artifacts in depth.

This ensures quality, scalability, and governance alignment.
