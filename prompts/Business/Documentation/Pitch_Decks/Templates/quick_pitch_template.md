## 30-Second Elevator Pitch

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** (5 sec): Grab attention with problem or stat
2. **Solution** (10 sec): What you do and for whom
3. **Traction/Proof** (10 sec): Key metric or achievement
4. **Ask/CTA** (5 sec): What you want from listener

**Example:**
> "[Problem stat]. We're [Company], and we [solution] for [target market]. We've already [traction metric], and we're raising [amount] to [goal]. [Would love to chat more / Can I send you our deck?]"

## 2-Minute Demo Day Pitch

**Structure (5 slides max):**

### Slide 1: Problem
- One powerful problem statement
- Market size
- Why now?

### Slide 2: Solution
- Your product in one sentence
- Key differentiator
- Visual of product if possible

### Slide 3: Traction
- Most impressive metric
- Growth rate
- Key milestone

### Slide 4: Market Opportunity
- TAM
- Your position/approach
- Go-to-market strategy

### Slide 5: Team & Ask
- Founder credentials (1-2 key points)
- Amount raising
- What this achieves
- Contact info

## 5-Minute Pitch (No Slides)

**Narrative Structure:**

**Opening (30 sec):**
- Introduce yourself and company
- Hook with problem or vision

**Problem (1 min):**
- Paint picture of pain point
- Quantify the impact
- Why existing solutions fail

**Solution (1.5 min):**
- Your product/service
- How it works (simply)
- Key benefits and differentiators
- Quick demo or example

**Market & Opportunity (1 min):**
- Market size and trends
- Target customer
- Business model basics
- Path to scale

**Traction & Team (1 min):**
- Key metrics and growth
- Major milestones
- Why your team will win
- What you're raising (if applicable)

**Close (30 sec):**
- Vision for future
- Clear call-to-action
- Thank you

## Tips for Each Format

### 30-Second Pitch
- **Memorize it:** Should flow naturally
- **Practice everywhere:** Elevator, coffee line, networking
- **Be conversational:** Not robotic
- **Have a hook:** Interesting stat or question
- **End with ask:** Make it easy for them to help

### 2-Minute Pitch
- **Practice timing:** 2 minutes is fast
- **Use visuals:** Images > text
- **Show momentum:** Growth chart if possible
- **Be passionate:** Energy matters
- **Nail the close:** Make the ask clear

### 5-Minute Pitch
- **Tell a story:** Connect emotionally
- **Use concrete examples:** Real customer stories
- **Show expertise:** Demonstrate domain knowledge
- **Read the room:** Adjust based on audience
- **Save time for questions:** Aim for 4:30 to allow Q&A

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Too much detail:** Keep it high-level  
❌ **Jargon overload:** Use simple language  
❌ **No clear ask:** Be explicit about what you want  
❌ **Overconfident:** Balance ambition with realism  
❌ **Ignoring audience:** Tailor to who's listening  
❌ **Memorized robot:** Sound natural and conversational  
❌ **Weak opening:** Hook them in first 10 seconds

## Variations by Context

### Networking Event
- Lead with problem/solution
- Keep it conversational
- End with "Can I send you more info?"

### Demo Day
- Focus on traction and growth
- Visual proof of product
- Clear funding ask

### Competition
- Emphasize uniqueness
- Show validation/traction
- Confidence without arrogance

### Customer Pitch
- Lead with their pain point
- Show ROI and benefits
- Include social proof/case study
