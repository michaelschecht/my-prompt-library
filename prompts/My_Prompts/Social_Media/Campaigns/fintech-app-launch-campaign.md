# Fintech App Launch Cross-Platform Campaign

## Purpose
Design a multi-channel social media launch campaign for a new consumer fintech application to drive initial app downloads and user registration.

## Instructions
Act as a digital marketing director for a fintech startup. Outline a 2-week launch campaign spanning Instagram, Twitter, and TikTok for a new consumer finance app (e.g., budgeting, investing, or peer-to-peer payments). The campaign should build trust, highlight ease of use, and leverage early-adopter incentives.

Specify the app's core feature, target demographic (e.g., Gen Z, young professionals), and the main launch incentive.

## Output Format
Structure the campaign plan as follows:
1. **Launch Strategy & Messaging Pillars**: Core message, target audience, and unique selling proposition (USP).
2. **Channel Strategy**: Specific goals and content styles for Instagram, Twitter, and TikTok.
3. **Phased Content Calendar**:
   - **Teaser Phase (Days 1-3)**
   - **Launch Day (Day 4)**
   - **Momentum Phase (Days 5-14)**
4. **Influencer/Creator Collaboration**: Brief for partnering with financial content creators.
5. **Success Metrics**: App store ranking goals, CPI (Cost Per Install), and engagement rates.

## Example
**Input:**
App Core Feature: Micro-investing with automated round-ups.
Target Demographic: Gen Z and young millennials with limited investing experience.
Launch Incentive: $10 bonus for the first 1,000 users who link a bank account.

**Output:**
### 1. Launch Strategy & Messaging Pillars
**Core Message:** "Investing made effortless. Start building wealth with your spare change."
**USP:** No confusing jargon, automatic investing, secure platform.

### 2. Channel Strategy
- **TikTok:** Short, relatable skits about spending habits and the "aha!" moment of automated saving.
- **Instagram:** Visually appealing UI teasers, carousel posts explaining how it works, and user testimonials.
- **Twitter:** Thread on financial literacy, real-time updates on remaining $10 bonuses.

*(Continues with phases, influencer briefs, etc.)*
