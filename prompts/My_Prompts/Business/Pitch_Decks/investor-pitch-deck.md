Create a compelling investor pitch deck for [COMPANY NAME], a [DESCRIPTION] company targeting [MARKET].

**Context:**

**Deck Structure (10-15 slides):**

### 1. Title Slide
- Company name and tagline
- Founder names and titles
- Contact information
- Date

### 2. Problem Statement
- What pain point does this solve?
- Market size and impact
- Current inadequate solutions
- Why now? (market timing)

### 3. Solution
- Your product/service
- Key features and benefits
- How it solves the problem uniquely
- Demo or visual representation

### 4. Market Opportunity
- Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
- Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)
- Market trends and growth projections

### 5. Product/Technology
- Product screenshots or demo
- Key features and differentiation
- Technology stack (if applicable)
- Roadmap highlights

### 6. Business Model
- Revenue streams
- Pricing strategy
- Unit economics (CAC, LTV, margins)
- Path to profitability

### 7. Traction & Milestones
- Key metrics (users, revenue, growth rate)
- Major achievements to date
- Customer testimonials or case studies
- Partnerships or pilot programs

### 8. Go-to-Market Strategy
- Customer acquisition channels
- Sales strategy
- Marketing approach
- Strategic partnerships

### 9. Competitive Landscape
- Competitor analysis (competitive matrix)
- Your unique positioning
- Barriers to entry you've built
- Why you will win

### 10. Team
- Founder backgrounds and expertise
- Key team members
- Advisors and board members
- Why this team is uniquely qualified

### 11. Financial Projections
- Revenue projections (3-5 years)
- Key assumptions
- Path to profitability
- Major expense categories

### 12. The Ask
- Amount raising
- Use of funds (breakdown)
- Milestones this round will achieve
- Timeline to next milestone/round

### 13. Closing/Vision Slide
- Long-term vision
- Impact potential
- Thank you + contact info

**Tone & Style:**
- Clear and concise
- Data-driven but compelling story
- Professional but authentic
- Confident without being arrogant
- Visually engaging (minimal text per slide)

**Key Principles:**
- One idea per slide
- Use visuals > text
- Tell a story, not just facts
- Show traction and momentum
- Make the ask clear

## Tips & Best Practices

### Do's
- **Start strong:** Hook them in the first 30 seconds
- **Show traction:** Real numbers beat projections
- **Know your metrics:** CAC, LTV, churn, burn rate
- **Tell a story:** Connect slides into a narrative arc
- **Practice:** Rehearse until delivery is natural
- **Anticipate questions:** Have backup slides ready
- **Keep it visual:** Images and charts > text walls
- **Be realistic:** Conservative projections gain trust

### Don'ts
- **Don't overload slides:** Keep text minimal
- **Don't ignore competition:** "No competitors" = red flag
- **Don't overpromise:** Be ambitious but believable
- **Don't rush financials:** This is where many get tripped up
- **Don't use jargon:** Clear language wins
- **Don't skip the ask:** Be direct about what you need
- **Don't go over time:** Respect the schedule

### Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Deck designed and polished (consistent theme)
- [ ] All numbers validated and sourced
- [ ] Team has practiced together
- [ ] Backup slides prepared for deep dives
- [ ] Demo ready (if live demo planned)
- [ ] Appendix with additional data
- [ ] Follow-up materials prepared (data room)

## Variations by Stage

### Pre-Seed/Seed
- **Focus:** Problem, solution, team, vision
- **Traction:** Early signals, pilot customers, waitlist
- **Financials:** High-level projections
- **Ask:** Runway to prove product-market fit

### Series A
- **Focus:** Traction, growth, unit economics
- **Traction:** Proven revenue or user growth
- **Financials:** Detailed metrics and projections
- **Ask:** Capital to scale proven model

### Series B+
- **Focus:** Market domination, execution, returns
- **Traction:** Strong revenue growth, market position
- **Financials:** Path to profitability, exit potential
- **Ask:** Capital to capture market leadership

## Common Investor Questions

Be prepared to answer:
- What's your customer acquisition cost and payback period?
- How defensible is your competitive advantage?
- What are your biggest risks?
- Why will you succeed where others have failed?
- What happens if [major competitor] enters your space?
- How much runway does this give you?
- What are your post-money valuation expectations?
- Who else is in your round?
